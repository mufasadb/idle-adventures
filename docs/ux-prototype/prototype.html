KEY UX DECISIONS SUMMARY

1. Expedition CTA is always the hero.
Town screen is a launch pad, not a dashboard. The primary button is always "Start Expedition" — everything else is secondary grid. This ensures the core loop stays front-and-centre even as town features expand.
2. Action budget makes sessions legible.
Players need to know before committing how long a session will be. Showing "24 actions ≈ 14 min" lets players fit the game into their real life. The budget bar during expedition gives ongoing progress feedback.
3. Active vs Passive is a first-class toggle.
This is the core identity trade-off. It's placed in Expedition Prep (not buried in settings) and shows both yield percentages. Players choose their engagement level on each trip, not once as a global preference.
4. Grid path preview before confirming.
Blue highlight shows the planned route, resource types along it, and remaining action budget. This "plan then commit" model reduces frustration and teaches the packing puzzle over time.
5. Minigames have an escape hatch.
The "Skip (70%)" option on every minigame prevents any screen from feeling like a trap. Players who want auto yield never feel punished. Players who engage earn a bonus — not players who disengage get penalised.
6. Bank shows undiscovered items greyed.
Locked items visible-but-greyed (not hidden) communicates there is more to discover without spoiling it. This creates curiosity loops: "what IS that 4th gem?" drives continued play.
