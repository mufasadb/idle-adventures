# Balance Levers

The POC ships with *feel-pass* values, not balanced ones. The discipline that keeps the long game open: **every tunable is a named, documented constant in `src/data/`, grouped by system, commented with what pulling it does.** Engine logic never hardcodes a number ŌĆö it reads a lever. Tuning = turning labelled dials, not hunting for stray `7`s.

## Lever groups

**Energy economy** ŌĆö how far/long a trip lasts. Stamina model (D41/dtv): energy is CURRENT stamina on a max/current bar; you embark at `MAX_ENERGY` and EAT food to refill toward max. Total reach Ōēł `MAX_ENERGY + ╬Ż(food restore ├Ś tentMult)`.
- `MAX_ENERGY` (300, 2026-07-06 dtv) ŌĆö the stamina ceiling; `expedition.energy` starts here on embark regardless of packed food, and `expedition.maxEnergy` = this + any gear bonus (see `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS`). Bumping it raises baseline reach; a difficulty re-tune for the extra reach is a playtest follow-up
- `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS{toolDefId}` (si7.2, 2026-07-08, new ŌĆö `canteen: 100`) ŌĆö extra maxEnergy granted by equipping the named tool; `energyCapOf(equipment)` sums these at embark. The `canteen` (recipe: `copper-ore├Ś2 + deer-hide├Ś1`) is the first capacity-gear item, raising the ceiling to 400 (`MAX_ENERGY + 100`). Raise the bonus to make the canteen a stronger progression lever; add more entries for higher-tier capacity gear (m0a scope). `canteen` is catalogued under `TOOL_CAPABILITY ("provision")` and `TOOL_QUALITY (1)` so it packs as a tool slot.
- `TENT_FOOD_MULTIPLIER` (1.5, 2026-07-06 dtv) ŌĆö a `tent` (durable "camp" tool) multiplies energy restored per food unit, so each ration goes 50% further. Tunable
- `ENERGY_PER_FOOD` ŌĆö default energy RESTORED per food unit eaten (fallback for `FOOD_ENERGY`)
- `FOOD_ENERGY{foodDefId}` (`ration` 80 / `trail-ration` **130** / `berries` 30 / `jam` 120 / `pemmican` **240** / `apple` **40** / `smoked-venison` **200** / `blubber-stew` **160**) ŌĆö RESTORE per food unit eaten (D41: was embark energy, now refill-per-unit). Three-rung food ladder (si7.2, D47): `ration` is the floor (do NOT drop below 80 ŌĆö risks the forage-only tundra sustainability floor); `trail-ration` compressed 160ŌåÆ**130** (compressed toward the ration floor; pemmican now occupies the upper tier); `pemmican` (**240**, recipe: `drake-hide├Ś1 + stale-berries├Ś2`) is the ONE proof tier-food ŌĆö higher-tier packs spend fewer slots on the same energy, making progression tangible. `eatToRefill` is waste-free (never overfills past max). **Auto-eat rule (m0a, D48, resolves si7.7):** auto-eat is **least-dense-first** ŌĆö repeatedly picks the lowest-`FOOD_ENERGY` unit whose boosted restore fits under `maxEnergy`; a too-dense unit is passed over as a reserve (not a blocker), so `berries`/`apple` are eaten before rations, rations before trail-rations, etc. **Manual `eat` (m0a, D48):** targets the MOST-dense unit (the reserve auto-eat leaves alone) and jumps energy TO `foodEnergy ├Ś tentMult`, which may exceed `maxEnergy` (over-full drains normally); web bar surfaces the surplus as `+N`. No tent-safe cap on food density. Absent = `ENERGY_PER_FOOD`. `berries`/`jam` (e3j) are the fresh/processed pair: `berries` is weak-but-immediate fresh forage (eat on the trail or lose it to staleness); `jam` is the processed payoff (1.5 rations/slot) ŌĆö see `FRESH_TO_STALE` below. **m0a fresh-food additions:** `apple` (40, woodland orchard `wood` nodes ŌĆö material defId = food defId, stales to `bruised-apple` at banking via `FRESH_TO_STALE`; `apple-jam` recipe: `bruised-apple ├Ś3 ŌåÆ jam`); `smoked-venison` (200, `rich-venison + salt` ŌĆö cross-biome elk├Śdesert pull, the manual-over-eat reserve); `blubber-stew` (160, `seal-blubber + ice-moss` ŌĆö tundra tier-food)
- **FreshŌåÆprocessed food (D42, e3j):** `FRESH_TO_STALE{freshDefId ŌåÆ staleDefId}` (`berries ŌåÆ stale-berries`) maps a fresh forage defId to the material it becomes if hauled home uneaten ŌĆö `endExpedition` (banking) converts any leftover fresh units at return, so eating fresh forage on-map is good NOW, while hauling it home stales it into a craftable input (`jam`'s recipe: `stale-berries ├Ś3 ŌåÆ jam`). Stale forms are materials, not food (`slotOf` never returns `"food"` for them), so they can't be packed back out for another eat-fresh loop ŌĆö the rule enforces itself with no extra check. This is the live "eat-now-or-haul-home" call the reach economy needs food to be.
- (retired) `BASE_ENERGY_FLOOR` ŌĆö removed by D41; its "recoverable short run" role is now "embark at `MAX_ENERGY`, just nothing to refill with"
- `TERRAIN_COST{plains 10, mud 15, ice 20, river 30, mountain Ōł×}` ŌĆö **absolute** step energy on a ├Ś10 scale (svz, graded movement); `Infinity` = the one hard gate (mountain), enabled by `climbing-pick`
- `MIN_STEP` (5) ŌĆö floor: a discounted step never costs less than this
- `TERRAIN_GATE{terrain ŌåÆ toolDefId ŌåÆ {enable?, discount?}}` ŌĆö gear modifiers: `enable` turns an impassable terrain finite (climbing-pick/mountain ŌåÆ 40); `discount` subtracts points (raft/river ŌłÆ20, waders/mud ŌłÆ5, ice-cleats/ice ŌłÆ15 ŌåÆ glide). Each tool = one slot
- `TRANSPORT_MULTIPLIER{transport ŌåÆ terrain ŌåÆ divisor}` ŌĆö **per-terrain** move-cost divisor (svz): horse fast on open ground (plains ├Ę2), wagon the ice answer (ice ├Ę2), mule slow-but-hauler (├Ę0.8); absent terrain / on-foot = ├Ę1

**Carry** ŌĆö loot vs supplies tension (UNIT-based since Phase 2 / pqp)
- `BASE_CARRY_SLOTS` ŌĆö inventory slots with NO backpack (**6** since pqp) ┬Ę `BACKPACK_SLOTS{tier}` ŌĆö total slots per backpack (replaces the base): `starter` 8 / `leather` 12 / `large-pack` 16 ┬Ę `STACK_CAP` ŌĆö max qty per **loot** stack (**5**); consumables/tools do NOT stack
- **pqp (2026-07-05, supersedes D23):** each food/potion/battleItem **unit** and **each tool** is one slot; only loot stacks. Food is eaten just-in-time (`food.digest`) and its slot frees mid-run; **uneaten food banks back** on return. So the squeeze is *temporal* ŌĆö cramped early (heavy food), roomy late ŌĆö and packing a supply is a live, visible loot slot given up. Caps were bumped (3ŌåÆ6, 4/6/8ŌåÆ8/12/16) for the ~5├Ś consumable pressure vs the old per-stack model. Re-tuning: keep the sustainability harness green (`test/harness-sustainability.test.ts`).
- **Carry sources stack (zhn, 2026-07-05):** `carryCap(equipment)` = backpack tier + `TRANSPORT_CARRY{transport}` (a beast/cart bonus: horse 2, mule 4, wagon 6) + `PANNIERS_SLOTS{panniers}` (4) ŌĆö panniers only count with a `BEAST_TRANSPORTS` (horse/mule), so a mule+panniers is a hauler and a wagon can't wear them. `panniers` is a new equipment slot (worn/free, durable, banks back).

**Gathering**
- `NODE_HARDNESS{nodeType}` ŌĆö energy cost numerator ┬Ę `TOOL_QUALITY{toolDefId}` ŌĆö cost divisor **AND tier** (D31): quality doubles as the max material tier the tool can work ┬Ę `GATHER_YIELD{nodeType}` ŌĆö qty per (one-shot) node
- `NODE_TOOL{nodeType}` ŌĆö required capability (herb = bare hands) ┬Ę `TOOL_CAPABILITY{toolDefId}` ŌĆö tiered tools are data-only (D21: all per node type, never per biome)
- `MATERIAL_TIER{materialDefId}` (D31, 2026-07-04) ŌĆö a material's tier; gather rejects `tool-too-weak` when the best held tool's quality < this. Absent = tier 1. This **one lever gates the whole tech tree** (no smelting step): coal/silver (T2) need an iron-pick, mithril (T3) needs a steel-pick. The visible-but-locked node is the pull up the ladder.
- Yield defIds come from `BIOMES{id}.materialTable`, stamped onto POIs at generation (D25) ŌĆö materials across 3 biomes feed one shared recipe tree (M5); tiered materials (coal/ironwood/drake-hide T2, mithril T3) are data additions

**Harvest sim + sustainability targets (si7.2, D47, 2026-07-08)** ŌĆö calibrated reference for food-economy tuning. `src/sim/harvest.ts` runs a monster-aware greedy walker (routes around live monsters via Dijkstra; never engages) at a given map tier and reports the fraction of gatherable POIs cleared per seed. Used to set and guard the food-economy targets.
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_TIER_TARGET` (**0.50**) ŌĆö fraction of gatherable POIs a reference walker clears on a tier-N map (measured with a pemmican loadout). Calibration rationale: 60% is the design aspiration; the no-optimal-router reference walker lands at ~50% ŌĆö a conservative floor; real players route better. Do NOT lower below 0.40 (tier food must buy real reach).
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_BASE_TARGET` (**0.25**) ŌĆö same walker at the base tier (tier 1). Set to ~half the tier target; the 2├Ś ratio is the invariant that proves tiered food earns slot efficiency, not just raw restore. Measured: tier=50%, base=25%, ratio=1.99 ŌĆö gate passed.
- Hard gate: `tier_fraction Ōēź 1.8 ├Ś base_fraction`. If a food/energy tuning pass breaks this, revisit `FOOD_ENERGY.pemmican` or `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS` before touching 2yn demand levers. Run `bun run sim harvest --seed <tier>` to re-measure.

**Combat**
- `PLAYER_BASE_HP` ┬Ę `DMG_ARMOUR_MATRIX[dmgType][armourType]` ŌĆö read BOTH ways: damage multiplier vs the monster's hide class going out, mitigation divisor per armour piece coming in (`defense ├Ę matrix`) ┬Ę `ARMOUR{pieceDefId} ŌåÆ {armourType, defense}` ┬Ę `WEAPONS{defId} ŌåÆ {dmgType, damage, tags}` ┬Ę `UNARMED_DAMAGE`
- **`MITIGATION_K` (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new ŌĆö 6)** ŌĆö the armour-power dial: incoming damage is now `dmgIn = max(CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN, dmg ├Ś MITIGATION_K/(MITIGATION_K + D))` where `D = ╬Ż(defense ├Ę matrix)` (**supersedes flat subtraction**). Higher K = armour matters less (mitigation asymptotes toward 0% as KŌåÆŌł×); lower K = armour matters more. At today's K=6, full iron plate Ōēł ŌłÆ50%, mithril Ōēł ŌłÆ70% ŌĆö meaningful but never zeroed to chip, closing the M7 F1 hole where plate floored the entire bestiary
- `AFFINITIES[{monsterTag, itemTag}]` + `AFFINITY_MULTIPLIER` ŌĆö the hidden discoverable layer (silverŌåöwerewolf, ironŌåöfae, garlicŌåövampire); never shown by perception, **discovered post-fight** via `matchup.affinityFired` (D38)
- `POTION_HEAL` (default) ┬Ę `POTION_HEAL_BY{potionDefId}` (2026-07-04, `potion` 10 / `greater-potion` 20; absent = `POTION_HEAL`) ┬Ę `AUTO_POTION_THRESHOLD` (fraction of base HP) ŌĆö since si7.1 this also drives **auto-quaff inside engagement exchanges** (mirrors `autoEat`'s threshold-driven refill; toggled per-run by `autoQuaff`, default true) ┬Ę `CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN` ŌĆö the "HP always drains" floor, both directions (now also the mitigation formula's floor, see `MITIGATION_K` above)
- `QUAFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) ŌĆö energy to drink a potion OUTSIDE an engagement (82r "quaff anywhere"); in-combat quaff stays energy-free (its cost is tempo ŌĆö si7.1's combat balance was calibrated with it). Raise to make between-fights healing a real budget call; at ~a plains step it's a convenience price ┬Ę `DON_DOFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) ŌĆö energy to don/doff one piece of gear on the map (rejected while engaged). Raise to make pre-fight gear swaps a routing commitment instead of a free toggle
- **Ranged combat (D45, 2026-07-07):** `ARROW_STACK_CAP` (**20**) ŌĆö arrows per inventory slot; ammo is the ONE deep-stacking consumable (`stackCapOf` ŌåÆ this cap; `consumableSlots` counts `ceil(units/cap)`), so a slot of arrows Ōēł 20 shots. Lower it to tighten the ammo-vs-food-vs-loot squeeze; raise it to cheapen bow logistics ┬Ę `ARROWS_PER_CRAFT` (**10**) ŌĆö arrows per craft batch (`pine-log + flint + feather` ŌåÆ generous, per the user's "three different things to grab" call). These two together set shots-per-expedition-of-effort ŌĆö the bow style's real cost dial. `AMMO[]` is the catalog list (`slotOf` ŌåÆ `"ammo"`). The opener (first exchange of a ranged engagement skips the retaliation) is a RULE, not a lever ŌĆö if it needs tuning, that's an evasion model (explicitly deferred). Arrows-out damage is `UNARMED_DAMAGE` (no new lever unless playtest wants one)
- `COMBAT_BUFF{battleItemDefId} ŌåÆ {damageAdd?, mitigationAdd?}` (bzd, 2026-07-05; consumption reworked 90j/D50, 2026-07-09) ŌĆö packed battle items, added into the engagement's `damageAdd`/`mitigationAdd` (`elixir-of-power` +2 dmg, `warding-draught` +3 mitigation). **Used MANUALLY mid-fight** via the `use-item` action (90j) ŌĆö no longer auto-consumed at engage; unused items bank back. The buff persists for the rest of THAT engagement only. The T3-fight-only reward branch; they cost inventory slots (pqp) and let a fought-up player beat the Wyrm without full mithril (┬¦4.3). `BATTLE_ITEM[]` is the catalog list (`slotOf` ŌåÆ `"battle-item"`). `resolveCombat` (atomic sim API) still models packed battle items as used at fight start, so sim/harness balance is numerically unchanged.
- `MONSTER_TIER_HP_CURVE` (si7.1: **8/16/28/54** by tier, steepened from the D34 48-HP boss baseline) / `MONSTER_TIER_DMG_CURVE` (si7.1: **4/8/14/24** by tier) ŌĆö read together with `MITIGATION_K` they set matchup-scaled toll bands at tier 1: a bare-kit good matchup costs Ōēł13% of `PLAYER_BASE_HP`, neutral Ōēł27%, bad Ōēź40% ŌĆö HP is a second run-budget, not a rounding error. Tier 4 (54 HP / 24 dmg) re-pins the D34 Wyrm gate: full mithril takes 9 strikes to kill it and eats 8 retaliations back, landing squarely on the 3-greater-potion threshold ┬Ę `MONSTERS{defId} ŌåÆ {tier, dmgType, armourType, tags}` ┬Ę `LOOT_TABLE{monster}` (fixed drops, optional `chance?` for rare bonus drops like `dragonheart`@0.2 ŌĆö determinism needs no RNG, `rollLoot` is seed-keyed) ┬Ę `BIOMES{id}.creatureTable` ŌĆö **si7.1: now WEIGHTED** (`Record<defId, number>`, same shape as `materialTable`), replacing the old uniform pick. Tier-1/2 trash dominates the weight mass; each biome's tier-4 boss sits at the bottom (tundra's `ancient-wyrm` is 1 of a 16-weight table ŌĆö properly rare, not a uniform 1-in-6). Roster is now complete per biome (tier-1 melee/ranged/magic spread): woodland gained `shell-beetle` (melee/plate) and turned `forest-bandit` ranged; desert gained `mirage-wisp` (magic/robe); tundra gained `ice-crab` (melee/plate) ŌĆö each drops a part (`beetle-shell`/`wisp-essence`/`crab-shell`) feeding a new recipe (`plate-boots-beetle`/`fire-staff-wisp`/`ration-crab`). **m0a mid-tier additions (D48):** `giant-elk` (woodland, T2, melee/light, weight 3 ŌĆö drops `rich-venison ├Ś2`; no elk-antler; venison feeds `smoked-venison`); `dust-djinn` (desert, T2, magic/robe, weight 3 ŌĆö bow-bait; drops `djinn-ember ├Ś1`, the gateway to `elixir-of-power-thistle` and `elixir-of-power` without farming vampires); `frost-hatchling` (tundra, T2, magic/robe, weight 3 ŌĆö wyrm herald, `dragon` tag = wyrmbane affinity target; drops `hatchling-scale ├Ś1` always + `map-scroll ├Ś1` @15%; `scale-jerky` recipe renders scale into 2├Ś rations)
- **Engagement model (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new)** ŌĆö combat is no longer atomic: `Expedition.combat?: Engagement` holds a live fight; `fight` runs ONE exchange per call (both sides swing once, not the whole bout); `flee` disengages at the cost of one parting hit taken **and resets the monster's HP** (no free disengage ŌĆö the monster is still there next time); `quaff` drinks a potion mid-engagement with no exchange spent. Non-combat actions (`move`/`gather`/`drop`/`drop-map`/`return`/`eat`) reject `engaged` while `combat` is set ŌĆö `flee` becomes the one always-available out (amends the D29 "`return` always legal" note, which no longer holds while engaged).
- `DETAIL_RADIUS` (2) + `VISION_RANGE_BONUS{toolDefId}` (spyglass +3) ŌĆö passive perception (9u9.2, D38): node KIND always visible; qualitative `detail` (species/material/tier/dmg+armour type ŌĆö **never the outcome**) resolves only within `DETAIL_RADIUS + ╬Ż bonuses` of the player. Future glasses/cartography/scent items slot into `VISION_RANGE_BONUS`. (Replaced `SCOUT_ENERGY_COST`/`SCOUT_RADIUS`/`SCOUT_TOOL`.)
- `SURVEY_ENERGY` (5, 54f/D51) ŌĆö energy to actively **survey** one POI at range with a vision-capability tool (`TOOL_CAPABILITY[t]==='vision'`), resolving its `detail` whole-map away and recording it in `Expedition.surveyed` (perceive then treats it as always-in-radius; it persists for the run). The spyglass's ACTIVE verb ŌĆö the price of studying the glass ŌĆö set at ~half a plains step. Raise to make each survey a real budget call, lower toward free to make a vision tool a pervasive detail-reveal. Range is deliberately the whole map; the energy + slot + not-engaged gate is the cost (revisit if a playtest shows abuse).

**Crafting**
- `RECIPE{itemDefId} ŌåÆ {inputs, output}` ŌĆö the shared tree (M5, filled): tiered tools (`iron-pick` halves mining cost ŌĆö the "cheaper second run" demonstrator), backpacks, weapons incl. affinity gear (`silver-sword`), armour pieces, transport, food, potions. Cross-biome inputs are a **soft pull** (D27: silver best-farmed in tundra, obtainable anywhere), not a hard gate. `FOOD`/`POTION` catalogs list which defIds `pack`/`slotOf` accept in the consumable slots.

**Map & forecast** ŌĆö where prep skill lives

- `BIOMES{id} ŌåÆ {terrainWeights, nodeTypeWeights, creatureTable, materialTable, barrierTerrain}` ŌĆö a biome is a **generation profile only** (D21): consumed by `generateGrid(mapSeed, biomeId)`, never consulted at runtime. (Subsumes the earlier flat `NOISE_THRESHOLDS` ŌĆö thresholds now live per-biome in `terrainWeights`.)
- POI positions sample walkable tiles only (walls carry no nodes) since e3j.
- **Held maps (D40, xzx, 2026-07-06):** `GameState.maps: MapItem[]` is the pocketed-map collection ŌĆö no lever this pass (`pocket-map` has **no cost and no cap**: "go nearby" means you're never gated, so hoarding buys nothing). - `CANDIDATE_MAP_COUNT` (3) ┬Ę `PREVIEW_FIDELITY` ŌĆö how much the map preview reveals beyond the biome-name headline (the master dial for how much preparation matters). Ships at **0** (biome-name headline only; `candidateMaps` returns empty `hints`); `town.previewHints` is structured so higher tiers ŌĆö and a later **cartography** system (craftable/editable maps) ŌĆö plug in without reshaping the preview.
- `MAP_DROP_CHANCE` (0.5, 8ec) ŌĆö humanoid-kill map-scroll drop rate (`CATEGORY_LOOT_TABLE.humanoid`). Regular but not guaranteed: humanoids reliably mean maps over a few kills without every kill printing one. Raise toward 1.0 if map supply feels starved; the carry-slot cost (each carried map = one slot for the run) is the real limiter.
- `EPITHETS` (q2k, D49) ŌĆö ordered notability table; a map whose generated content crosses a threshold gets its label appended to the display name ("ŌĆ” of carbon"). This is the "how often is a map notable" dial ŌĆö **raise** a `minCount`/`minShare`/`creatureTierAtLeast` to make that epithet rarer (fewer named maps), **lower** it to name more maps; keep the whole sweep under ~40% named or epithets stop being notable (`test/epithet.test.ts` asserts this on a T1 offer sweep). **Map tiers (2yn, D46, 2026-07-08)** ŌĆö difficulty/density/loot ramp applied by `tierProfile` at grid generation. T1 = identity (no transform). All levers below are in `src/data/constants.ts`.
- `MAP_TIER_MAX` (**5**) ŌĆö deepest tier; `min(sourceTier+1, MAP_TIER_MAX)` caps the drop-ladder. Raise to add more rungs; lower to compress progression.
- `POI_DENSITY_BY_TIER` ŌĆö POI count override per tier (absent tier uses `POI_DENSITY`). Current: T2 +2, T3 +4, T4 +6, T5 +8. Richer maps get denser nodes; raise the deltas to make high-tier maps feel significantly more rewarding, lower to make POI count a flat property of the strip.
- `MATERIAL_TIER_WEIGHT` ŌĆö per-material weight multiplier by map tier. Sparse: absent defId/tier = 1. Today: `coal` ramps to 4├Ś at T4, `iron-ore` peaks at 2├Ś T3ŌĆō4, `mithril-ore` unlocks at T3 and reaches 3├Ś at T5. This is the primary loot-quality dial per tier ŌĆö raise a weight to make that material more prevalent; setting a defId's T1 entry to anything other than 1 breaks the identity contract (asserted by test).
- `NODE_MAGNITUDE_WEIGHTS` ŌĆö distribution over yield classes {1,2,3} by tier. T1 = `{1:1}` (always base). Higher tiers shift mass toward class 3. Current: T5 weights are `{1:2, 2:4, 3:4}` ŌĆö about 40% chance of a 3├Ś node. Raise the class-3 weight to make rich nodes more common; setting T1 to anything other than `{1:1}` breaks the identity contract.
- `NODE_MAGNITUDE_YIELD` ŌĆö yield multiplier per magnitude class: class 1 = 1├Ś, class 2 = 2├Ś, class 3 = 3├Ś. These multiply `GATHER_YIELD[kind]`; adjust them to change how dramatic the rich-node bonus feels (flatten to make magnitude a flavour label, steepen to make T5 rich nodes the dominant supply source).
- `MAP_TIER_CREATURE_ADD` ŌĆö **biome-scoped** boss gate (the SINGLE source of boss spawns): `Record<BiomeId, Record<tier, Record<defId, weight>>>`. Each entry is the FULL weight-add for that biome+tier (not a delta). Bosses are removed from the base `BIOMES[*].creatureTable` and live exclusively here ŌĆö `tierProfile` merges this layer on top. Current gates: desert `dust-vampire` from T2 (weight 1ŌåÆ2ŌåÆ2ŌåÆ3 at T2ŌĆō5); tundra `ice-troll` from T2, `ancient-wyrm` from T3 (graduated minibossŌåÆwyrm arc, each weight ramps with tier). Adding a boss = add its defId to its native biome's entries here (never to `creatureTable`). Raise a weight to make a boss more common within the tier; add it at a lower tier to gate it earlier.
- `TERRAIN_WEIGHT_TIER_SHIFT` ŌĆö per-terrain weight multiplier by tier. Absent tier/terrain = 1. Current: mountain and river get progressively heavier from T2ŌåÆT5 (1.15ŌåÆ1.7), ice follows from T3. This is the energy-cost ramp: higher weights make obstacle terrain more prevalent, forcing longer routes and more food. Raise shifts to make high-tier maps punishing to navigate; lower to let the creature/loot ramp carry difficulty without geography.
