# Balance Levers

The POC ships with *feel-pass* values, not balanced ones. The discipline that keeps the long game open: **every tunable is a named, documented constant in `src/data/`, grouped by system, commented with what pulling it does.** Engine logic never hardcodes a number ÔÇö it reads a lever. Tuning = turning labelled dials, not hunting for stray `7`s.

## Lever groups

**Energy economy** ÔÇö how far/long a trip lasts. Stamina model (D41/dtv): energy is CURRENT stamina on a max/current bar; you embark at `MAX_ENERGY` and EAT food to refill toward max. Total reach Ôëł `MAX_ENERGY + ╬ú(food restore ├Ś tentMult)`.
- `MAX_ENERGY` (300, 2026-07-06 dtv) ÔÇö the stamina ceiling; `expedition.energy` starts here on embark regardless of packed food, and `expedition.maxEnergy` = this + any gear bonus (see `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS`). Bumping it raises baseline reach; a difficulty re-tune for the extra reach is a playtest follow-up
- `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS{toolDefId}` (si7.2, 2026-07-08, new ÔÇö `canteen: 100`) ÔÇö extra maxEnergy granted by equipping the named tool; `energyCapOf(equipment)` sums these at embark. The `canteen` (recipe: `copper-ore├Ś2 + deer-hide├Ś1`) is the first capacity-gear item, raising the ceiling to 400 (`MAX_ENERGY + 100`). Raise the bonus to make the canteen a stronger progression lever; add more entries for higher-tier capacity gear (m0a scope). `canteen` is catalogued under `TOOL_CAPABILITY ("provision")` and `TOOL_QUALITY (1)` so it packs as a tool slot.
- `TENT_FOOD_MULTIPLIER` (1.5, 2026-07-06 dtv) ÔÇö a `tent` (durable "camp" tool) multiplies energy restored per food unit, so each ration goes 50% further. Tunable
- `ENERGY_PER_FOOD` ÔÇö default energy RESTORED per food unit eaten (fallback for `FOOD_ENERGY`)
- `FOOD_ENERGY{foodDefId}` (`ration` 80 / `trail-ration` **130** / `berries` 30 / `jam` 120 / `pemmican` **240** / `apple` **40** / `smoked-venison` **200** / `blubber-stew` **160**) ÔÇö RESTORE per food unit eaten (D41: was embark energy, now refill-per-unit). Three-rung food ladder (si7.2, D47): `ration` is the floor (do NOT drop below 80 ÔÇö risks the forage-only tundra sustainability floor); `trail-ration` compressed 160Ôćĺ**130** (compressed toward the ration floor; pemmican now occupies the upper tier); `pemmican` (**240**, recipe: `drake-hide├Ś1 + stale-berries├Ś2`) is the ONE proof tier-food ÔÇö higher-tier packs spend fewer slots on the same energy, making progression tangible. `eatToRefill` is waste-free (never overfills past max). **Auto-eat rule (m0a, D48, resolves si7.7):** auto-eat is **least-dense-first** ÔÇö repeatedly picks the lowest-`FOOD_ENERGY` unit whose boosted restore fits under `maxEnergy`; a too-dense unit is passed over as a reserve (not a blocker), so `berries`/`apple` are eaten before rations, rations before trail-rations, etc. **Manual `eat` (m0a, D48):** targets the MOST-dense unit (the reserve auto-eat leaves alone) and jumps energy TO `foodEnergy ├Ś tentMult`, which may exceed `maxEnergy` (over-full drains normally); web bar surfaces the surplus as `+N`. No tent-safe cap on food density. Absent = `ENERGY_PER_FOOD`. `berries`/`jam` (e3j) are the fresh/processed pair: `berries` is weak-but-immediate fresh forage (eat on the trail or lose it to staleness); `jam` is the processed payoff (1.5 rations/slot) ÔÇö see `FRESH_TO_STALE` below. **m0a fresh-food additions:** `apple` (40, woodland orchard `wood` nodes ÔÇö material defId = food defId, stales to `bruised-apple` at banking via `FRESH_TO_STALE`; `apple-jam` recipe: `bruised-apple ├Ś3 Ôćĺ jam`); `smoked-venison` (200, `rich-venison + salt` ÔÇö cross-biome elk├Śdesert pull, the manual-over-eat reserve); `blubber-stew` (160, `seal-blubber + ice-moss` ÔÇö tundra tier-food)
- **FreshÔćĺprocessed food (D42, e3j):** `FRESH_TO_STALE{freshDefId Ôćĺ staleDefId}` (`berries Ôćĺ stale-berries`) maps a fresh forage defId to the material it becomes if hauled home uneaten ÔÇö `endExpedition` (banking) converts any leftover fresh units at return, so eating fresh forage on-map is good NOW, while hauling it home stales it into a craftable input (`jam`'s recipe: `stale-berries ├Ś3 Ôćĺ jam`). Stale forms are materials, not food (`slotOf` never returns `"food"` for them), so they can't be packed back out for another eat-fresh loop ÔÇö the rule enforces itself with no extra check. This is the live "eat-now-or-haul-home" call the reach economy needs food to be.
- (retired) `BASE_ENERGY_FLOOR` ÔÇö removed by D41; its "recoverable short run" role is now "embark at `MAX_ENERGY`, just nothing to refill with"
- `TERRAIN_COST{plains 10, mud 15, ice 20, river 30, mountain Ôł×}` ÔÇö **absolute** step energy on a ├Ś10 scale (svz, graded movement); `Infinity` = the one hard gate (mountain), enabled by `climbing-pick`
- `MIN_STEP` (5) ÔÇö floor: a discounted step never costs less than this
- `TERRAIN_GATE{terrain Ôćĺ toolDefId Ôćĺ {enable?, discount?}}` ÔÇö gear modifiers: `enable` turns an impassable terrain finite (climbing-pick/mountain Ôćĺ 40); `discount` subtracts points (raft/river Ôłĺ20, waders/mud Ôłĺ5, ice-cleats/ice Ôłĺ15 Ôćĺ glide). Each tool = one slot
- `TRANSPORT_MULTIPLIER{transport Ôćĺ terrain Ôćĺ divisor}` ÔÇö **per-terrain** move-cost divisor (svz): horse fast on open ground (plains ├Ě2), wagon the ice answer (ice ├Ě2), mule slow-but-hauler (├Ě0.8); absent terrain / on-foot = ├Ě1

**Carry** ÔÇö loot vs supplies tension (UNIT-based since Phase 2 / pqp)
- `BASE_CARRY_SLOTS` ÔÇö inventory slots with NO backpack (**6** since pqp) ┬Ě `BACKPACK_SLOTS{tier}` ÔÇö total slots per backpack (replaces the base): `starter` 8 / `leather` 12 / `large-pack` 16 ┬Ě `STACK_CAP` ÔÇö max qty per **loot** stack (**5**); consumables/tools do NOT stack
- **pqp (2026-07-05, supersedes D23):** each food/potion/battleItem **unit** and **each tool** is one slot; only loot stacks. Food is eaten just-in-time (`food.digest`) and its slot frees mid-run; **uneaten food banks back** on return. So the squeeze is *temporal* ÔÇö cramped early (heavy food), roomy late ÔÇö and packing a supply is a live, visible loot slot given up. Caps were bumped (3Ôćĺ6, 4/6/8Ôćĺ8/12/16) for the ~5├Ś consumable pressure vs the old per-stack model. Re-tuning: keep the sustainability harness green (`test/harness-sustainability.test.ts`).
- **Carry sources stack (zhn, 2026-07-05):** `carryCap(equipment)` = backpack tier + `TRANSPORT_CARRY{transport}` (a beast/cart bonus: horse 2, mule 4, wagon 6) + `PANNIERS_SLOTS{panniers}` (4) ÔÇö panniers only count with a `BEAST_TRANSPORTS` (horse/mule), so a mule+panniers is a hauler and a wagon can't wear them. `panniers` is a new equipment slot (worn/free, durable, banks back).

**Gathering**
- `NODE_HARDNESS{nodeType}` ÔÇö energy cost numerator ┬Ě `TOOL_QUALITY{toolDefId}` ÔÇö cost divisor **AND tier** (D31): quality doubles as the max material tier the tool can work ┬Ě `GATHER_YIELD{nodeType}` ÔÇö qty per (one-shot) node
- `NODE_TOOL{nodeType}` ÔÇö required capability (herb = bare hands) ┬Ě `TOOL_CAPABILITY{toolDefId}` ÔÇö tiered tools are data-only (D21: all per node type, never per biome)
- `MATERIAL_TIER{materialDefId}` (D31, 2026-07-04) ÔÇö a material's tier; gather rejects `tool-too-weak` when the best held tool's quality < this. Absent = tier 1. This **one lever gates the whole tech tree** (no smelting step): coal/silver (T2) need an iron-pick, mithril (T3) needs a steel-pick. The visible-but-locked node is the pull up the ladder.
- Yield defIds come from `BIOMES{id}.materialTable`, stamped onto POIs at generation (D25) ÔÇö materials across 3 biomes feed one shared recipe tree (M5); tiered materials (coal/ironwood/drake-hide T2, mithril T3) are data additions

**Harvest sim + sustainability targets (si7.2, D47, 2026-07-08)** ÔÇö calibrated reference for food-economy tuning. `src/sim/harvest.ts` runs a monster-aware greedy walker (routes around live monsters via Dijkstra; never engages) at a given map tier and reports the fraction of gatherable POIs cleared per seed. Used to set and guard the food-economy targets.
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_TIER_TARGET` (**0.50**) ÔÇö fraction of gatherable POIs a reference walker clears on a tier-N map (measured with a pemmican loadout). Calibration rationale: 60% is the design aspiration; the no-optimal-router reference walker lands at ~50% ÔÇö a conservative floor; real players route better. Do NOT lower below 0.40 (tier food must buy real reach).
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_BASE_TARGET` (**0.25**) ÔÇö same walker at the base tier (tier 1). Set to ~half the tier target; the 2├Ś ratio is the invariant that proves tiered food earns slot efficiency, not just raw restore. Measured: tier=50%, base=25%, ratio=1.99 ÔÇö gate passed.
- Hard gate: `tier_fraction Ôëą 1.8 ├Ś base_fraction`. If a food/energy tuning pass breaks this, revisit `FOOD_ENERGY.pemmican` or `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS` before touching 2yn demand levers. Run `bun run sim harvest --seed <tier>` to re-measure.

**Combat**
- `PLAYER_BASE_HP` ┬Ě `DMG_ARMOUR_MATRIX[dmgType][armourType]` ÔÇö read BOTH ways: damage multiplier vs the monster's hide class going out, mitigation divisor per armour piece coming in (`defense ├Ě matrix`) ┬Ě `ARMOUR{pieceDefId} Ôćĺ {armourType, defense}` ┬Ě `WEAPONS{defId} Ôćĺ {dmgType, damage, tags}` ┬Ě `UNARMED_DAMAGE`
- **`MITIGATION_K` (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new ÔÇö 6)** ÔÇö the armour-power dial: incoming damage is now `dmgIn = max(CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN, dmg ├Ś MITIGATION_K/(MITIGATION_K + D))` where `D = ╬ú(defense ├Ě matrix)` (**supersedes flat subtraction**). Higher K = armour matters less (mitigation asymptotes toward 0% as KÔćĺÔł×); lower K = armour matters more. At today's K=6, full iron plate Ôëł Ôłĺ50%, mithril Ôëł Ôłĺ70% ÔÇö meaningful but never zeroed to chip, closing the M7 F1 hole where plate floored the entire bestiary
- `AFFINITIES[{monsterTag, itemTag}]` + `AFFINITY_MULTIPLIER` ÔÇö the hidden discoverable layer (silverÔćöwerewolf, ironÔćöfae, garlicÔćövampire); never shown by perception, **discovered post-fight** via `matchup.affinityFired` (D38)
- `POTION_HEAL` (default) ┬Ě `POTION_HEAL_BY{potionDefId}` (2026-07-04, `potion` 10 / `greater-potion` 20; absent = `POTION_HEAL`) ┬Ě `AUTO_POTION_THRESHOLD` (fraction of base HP) ÔÇö since si7.1 this also drives **auto-quaff inside engagement exchanges** (mirrors `autoEat`'s threshold-driven refill; toggled per-run by `autoQuaff`, default true) ┬Ě `CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN` ÔÇö the "HP always drains" floor, both directions (now also the mitigation formula's floor, see `MITIGATION_K` above)
- `QUAFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) ÔÇö energy to drink a potion OUTSIDE an engagement (82r "quaff anywhere"); in-combat quaff stays energy-free (its cost is tempo ÔÇö si7.1's combat balance was calibrated with it). Raise to make between-fights healing a real budget call; at ~a plains step it's a convenience price ┬Ě `DON_DOFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) ÔÇö energy to don/doff one piece of gear on the map (rejected while engaged). Raise to make pre-fight gear swaps a routing commitment instead of a free toggle
- **Ranged combat (D45, 2026-07-07):** `ARROW_STACK_CAP` (**20**) ÔÇö arrows per inventory slot; ammo is the ONE deep-stacking consumable (`stackCapOf` Ôćĺ this cap; `consumableSlots` counts `ceil(units/cap)`), so a slot of arrows Ôëł 20 shots. Lower it to tighten the ammo-vs-food-vs-loot squeeze; raise it to cheapen bow logistics ┬Ě `ARROWS_PER_CRAFT` (**10**) ÔÇö arrows per craft batch (`pine-log + flint + feather` Ôćĺ generous, per the user's "three different things to grab" call). The opener (first exchange of a ranged engagement skips the retaliation) is a RULE, not a lever ÔÇö if it needs tuning, that's an evasion model (explicitly deferred). Arrows-out damage is `UNARMED_DAMAGE` (no new lever unless playtest wants one) ┬Ě **Fletching yield-mod (ke3.3/D54, 2026-07-09):** `ARROW_SHAFTS_PER_LOG` (**3**) ÔÇö the `arrow-shaft` recipe's `outputScale.qtyPer`; shafts per oak-log = this ├Ś the fletch tool's `TOOL_QUALITY` (`fletchers-knife` q1 Ôćĺ 3, `steel-fletchers-knife` q2 Ôćĺ 6). `arrows-fletched` consumes `ARROW_SHAFTS_PER_LOG` shafts + flint + feather Ôćĺ `ARROWS_PER_CRAFT` arrows, so at the base knife one log = one batch (parallel to the direct `arrows` recipe ÔÇö no free lunch), and the steel knife HALVES wood/arrow (the visible tool payoff, repays 57l). - **Engagement model (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new)** ÔÇö combat is no longer atomic: `Expedition.combat?: Engagement` holds a live fight; `fight` runs ONE exchange per call (both sides swing once, not the whole bout); `flee` disengages at the cost of one parting hit taken **and resets the monster's HP** (no free disengage ÔÇö the monster is still there next time); `quaff` drinks a potion mid-engagement with no exchange spent. Non-combat actions (`move`/`gather`/`drop`/`drop-map`/`return`/`eat`) reject `engaged` while `combat` is set ÔÇö `flee` becomes the one always-available out (amends the D29 "`return` always legal" note, which no longer holds while engaged).

- `TOOL_PURPOSE{capability}` (D59, 2026-07-09, gate-legibility) ÔÇö **display-only, not a balance dial:** maps a tool CAPABILITY to a short "what door it opens" clause (heatÔćĺ"enables field cooking", alchemyÔćĺ"enables field brewing", smithÔćĺ"forges metal plate at an anvil", visionÔćĺ"reveals a far node's material tier when you survey it"). **Crafting**
- `RECIPE{itemDefId} Ôćĺ {inputs, output}` ÔÇö the shared tree (M5, filled): tiered tools (`iron-pick` halves mining cost ÔÇö the "cheaper second run" demonstrator), backpacks, weapons incl. affinity gear (`silver-sword`), armour pieces, transport, food, potions. Cross-biome inputs are a **soft pull** (D27: silver best-farmed in tundra, obtainable anywhere), not a hard gate. `FOOD`/`POTION` catalogs list which defIds `pack`/`slotOf` accept in the consumable slots.

**Map tiers (2yn, D46, 2026-07-08)** ÔÇö difficulty/density/loot ramp applied by `tierProfile` at grid generation. T1 = identity (no transform). All levers below are in `src/data/constants.ts`.
- `MAP_TIER_MAX` (**5**) ÔÇö deepest tier; `min(sourceTier+1, MAP_TIER_MAX)` caps the drop-ladder. Raise to add more rungs; lower to compress progression.
- `POI_DENSITY_BY_TIER` ÔÇö POI count override per tier (absent tier uses `POI_DENSITY`). Current: T2 +2, T3 +4, T4 +6, T5 +8. Richer maps get denser nodes; raise the deltas to make high-tier maps feel significantly more rewarding, lower to make POI count a flat property of the strip.
- `MATERIAL_TIER_WEIGHT` ÔÇö per-material weight multiplier by map tier. Sparse: absent defId/tier = 1. - `NODE_MAGNITUDE_WEIGHTS` ÔÇö distribution over yield classes {1,2,3} by tier. T1 = `{1:1}` (always base). Higher tiers shift mass toward class 3. - `MAP_TIER_CREATURE_ADD` ÔÇö **biome-scoped** boss gate (the SINGLE source of boss spawns): `Record<BiomeId, Record<tier, Record<defId, weight>>>`. Each entry is the FULL weight-add for that biome+tier (not a delta). Bosses are removed from the base `BIOMES[*].creatureTable` and live exclusively here ÔÇö `tierProfile` merges this layer on top. Current gates: desert `dust-vampire` from T2 (weight 1Ôćĺ2Ôćĺ2Ôćĺ3 at T2ÔÇô5); tundra `ice-troll` from T2, `ancient-wyrm` from T3 (graduated minibossÔćĺwyrm arc, each weight ramps with tier). - `TERRAIN_WEIGHT_TIER_SHIFT` ÔÇö per-terrain weight multiplier by tier. Absent tier/terrain = 1. Current: mountain and river get progressively heavier from T2ÔćĺT5 (1.15Ôćĺ1.7), ice follows from T3.
