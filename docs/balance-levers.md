# Balance Levers

The POC ships with *feel-pass* values, not balanced ones. The discipline that keeps the long game open: **every tunable is a named, documented constant in `src/data/`, grouped by system, commented with what pulling it does.** Engine logic never hardcodes a number ‚Äî it reads a lever. Tuning = turning labelled dials, not hunting for stray `7`s.

## Lever groups

**Energy economy** ‚Äî how far/long a trip lasts. Stamina model (D41/dtv): energy is CURRENT stamina on a max/current bar; you embark at `MAX_ENERGY` and EAT food to refill toward max. Total reach ‚âà `MAX_ENERGY + Œ£(food restore √ó tentMult)`.
- `MAX_ENERGY` (300, 2026-07-06 dtv) ‚Äî the stamina ceiling; `expedition.energy` starts here on embark regardless of packed food, and `expedition.maxEnergy` = this + any gear bonus (see `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS`). Bumping it raises baseline reach; a difficulty re-tune for the extra reach is a playtest follow-up
- `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS{toolDefId}` (si7.2, 2026-07-08, new ‚Äî `canteen: 100`) ‚Äî extra maxEnergy granted by equipping the named tool; `energyCapOf(equipment)` sums these at embark. The `canteen` (recipe: `copper-ore√ó2 + deer-hide√ó1`) is the first capacity-gear item, raising the ceiling to 400 (`MAX_ENERGY + 100`). Raise the bonus to make the canteen a stronger progression lever; add more entries for higher-tier capacity gear (m0a scope). `canteen` is catalogued under `TOOL_CAPABILITY ("provision")` and `TOOL_QUALITY (1)` so it packs as a tool slot.
- `TENT_FOOD_MULTIPLIER` (1.5, 2026-07-06 dtv) ‚Äî a `tent` (durable "camp" tool) multiplies energy restored per food unit, so each ration goes 50% further. Tunable
- `ENERGY_PER_FOOD` ‚Äî default energy RESTORED per food unit eaten (fallback for `FOOD_ENERGY`)
- `FOOD_ENERGY{foodDefId}` (`ration` 80 / `trail-ration` **130** / `berries` 30 / `jam` 120 / `pemmican` **240** / `apple` **40** / `smoked-venison` **200** / `blubber-stew` **160**) ‚Äî RESTORE per food unit eaten (D41: was embark energy, now refill-per-unit). Three-rung food ladder (si7.2, D47): `ration` is the floor (do NOT drop below 80 ‚Äî risks the forage-only tundra sustainability floor); `trail-ration` compressed 160‚Üí**130** (compressed toward the ration floor; pemmican now occupies the upper tier); `pemmican` (**240**, recipe: `drake-hide√ó1 + stale-berries√ó2`) is the ONE proof tier-food ‚Äî higher-tier packs spend fewer slots on the same energy, making progression tangible. `eatToRefill` is waste-free (never overfills past max). **Auto-eat rule (m0a, D48, resolves si7.7):** auto-eat is **least-dense-first** ‚Äî repeatedly picks the lowest-`FOOD_ENERGY` unit whose boosted restore fits under `maxEnergy`; a too-dense unit is passed over as a reserve (not a blocker), so `berries`/`apple` are eaten before rations, rations before trail-rations, etc. **Manual `eat` (m0a, D48):** targets the MOST-dense unit (the reserve auto-eat leaves alone) and jumps energy TO `foodEnergy √ó tentMult`, which may exceed `maxEnergy` (over-full drains normally); web bar surfaces the surplus as `+N`. No tent-safe cap on food density. Absent = `ENERGY_PER_FOOD`. `berries`/`jam` (e3j) are the fresh/processed pair: `berries` is weak-but-immediate fresh forage (eat on the trail or lose it to staleness); `jam` is the processed payoff (1.5 rations/slot) ‚Äî see `FRESH_TO_STALE` below. **m0a fresh-food additions:** `apple` (40, woodland orchard `wood` nodes ‚Äî material defId = food defId, stales to `bruised-apple` at banking via `FRESH_TO_STALE`; `apple-jam` recipe: `bruised-apple √ó3 ‚Üí jam`); `smoked-venison` (200, `rich-venison + salt` ‚Äî cross-biome elk√ódesert pull, the manual-over-eat reserve); `blubber-stew` (160, `seal-blubber + ice-moss` ‚Äî tundra tier-food)
- **Fresh‚Üíprocessed food (D42, e3j):** `FRESH_TO_STALE{freshDefId ‚Üí staleDefId}` (`berries ‚Üí stale-berries`) maps a fresh forage defId to the material it becomes if hauled home uneaten ‚Äî `endExpedition` (banking) converts any leftover fresh units at return, so eating fresh forage on-map is good NOW, while hauling it home stales it into a craftable input (`jam`'s recipe: `stale-berries √ó3 ‚Üí jam`). Stale forms are materials, not food (`slotOf` never returns `"food"` for them), so they can't be packed back out for another eat-fresh loop ‚Äî the rule enforces itself with no extra check. This is the live "eat-now-or-haul-home" call the reach economy needs food to be.
- (retired) `BASE_ENERGY_FLOOR` ‚Äî removed by D41; its "recoverable short run" role is now "embark at `MAX_ENERGY`, just nothing to refill with"
- `TERRAIN_COST{plains 10, mud 15, ice 20, river 30, mountain ‚àû}` ‚Äî **absolute** step energy on a √ó10 scale (svz, graded movement); `Infinity` = the one hard gate (mountain), enabled by `climbing-pick`
- `MIN_STEP` (5) ‚Äî floor: a discounted step never costs less than this
- `TERRAIN_GATE{terrain ‚Üí toolDefId ‚Üí {enable?, discount?}}` ‚Äî gear modifiers: `enable` turns an impassable terrain finite (climbing-pick/mountain ‚Üí 40); `discount` subtracts points (raft/river ‚àí20, waders/mud ‚àí5, ice-cleats/ice ‚àí15 ‚Üí glide). Each tool = one slot
- `TRANSPORT_MULTIPLIER{transport ‚Üí terrain ‚Üí divisor}` ‚Äî **per-terrain** move-cost divisor (svz): horse fast on open ground (plains √∑2), wagon the ice answer (ice √∑2), mule slow-but-hauler (√∑0.8); absent terrain / on-foot = √∑1

**Carry** ‚Äî loot vs supplies tension (UNIT-based since Phase 2 / pqp)
- `BASE_CARRY_SLOTS` ‚Äî inventory slots with NO backpack (**6** since pqp) ¬∑ `BACKPACK_SLOTS{tier}` ‚Äî total slots per backpack (replaces the base): `starter` 8 / `leather` 12 / `large-pack` 16 ¬∑ `STACK_CAP` ‚Äî max qty per **loot** stack (**5**); consumables/tools do NOT stack
- **pqp (2026-07-05, supersedes D23):** each food/potion/battleItem **unit** and **each tool** is one slot; only loot stacks. Food is eaten just-in-time (`food.digest`) and its slot frees mid-run; **uneaten food banks back** on return. So the squeeze is *temporal* ‚Äî cramped early (heavy food), roomy late ‚Äî and packing a supply is a live, visible loot slot given up. Caps were bumped (3‚Üí6, 4/6/8‚Üí8/12/16) for the ~5√ó consumable pressure vs the old per-stack model. Re-tuning: keep the sustainability harness green (`test/harness-sustainability.test.ts`).
- **Carry sources stack (zhn, 2026-07-05):** `carryCap(equipment)` = backpack tier + `TRANSPORT_CARRY{transport}` (a beast/cart bonus: horse 2, mule 4, wagon 6) + `PANNIERS_SLOTS{panniers}` (4) ‚Äî panniers only count with a `BEAST_TRANSPORTS` (horse/mule), so a mule+panniers is a hauler and a wagon can't wear them. `panniers` is a new equipment slot (worn/free, durable, banks back).

**Gathering**
- `NODE_HARDNESS{nodeType}` ‚Äî energy cost numerator ¬∑ `TOOL_QUALITY{toolDefId}` ‚Äî cost divisor **AND tier** (D31): quality doubles as the max material tier the tool can work ¬∑ `GATHER_YIELD{nodeType}` ‚Äî qty per (one-shot) node
- `NODE_TOOL{nodeType}` ‚Äî required capability (herb = bare hands) ¬∑ `TOOL_CAPABILITY{toolDefId}` ‚Äî tiered tools are data-only (D21: all per node type, never per biome)
- `MATERIAL_TIER{materialDefId}` (D31, 2026-07-04) ‚Äî a material's tier; gather rejects `tool-too-weak` when the best held tool's quality < this. Absent = tier 1. This **one lever gates the whole tech tree** (no smelting step): coal/silver (T2) need an iron-pick, mithril (T3) needs a steel-pick. The visible-but-locked node is the pull up the ladder.
- Yield defIds come from `BIOMES{id}.materialTable`, stamped onto POIs at generation (D25) ‚Äî materials across 3 biomes feed one shared recipe tree (M5); tiered materials (coal/ironwood/drake-hide T2, mithril T3) are data additions

**Harvest sim + sustainability targets (si7.2, D47, 2026-07-08)** ‚Äî calibrated reference for food-economy tuning. `src/sim/harvest.ts` runs a monster-aware greedy walker (routes around live monsters via Dijkstra; never engages) at a given map tier and reports the fraction of gatherable POIs cleared per seed. Used to set and guard the food-economy targets.
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_TIER_TARGET` (**0.50**) ‚Äî fraction of gatherable POIs a reference walker clears on a tier-N map (measured with a pemmican loadout). Calibration rationale: 60% is the design aspiration; the no-optimal-router reference walker lands at ~50% ‚Äî a conservative floor; real players route better. Do NOT lower below 0.40 (tier food must buy real reach).
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_BASE_TARGET` (**0.25**) ‚Äî same walker at the base tier (tier 1). Set to ~half the tier target; the 2√ó ratio is the invariant that proves tiered food earns slot efficiency, not just raw restore. Measured: tier=50%, base=25%, ratio=1.99 ‚Äî gate passed.
- Hard gate: `tier_fraction ‚â• 1.8 √ó base_fraction`. If a food/energy tuning pass breaks this, revisit `FOOD_ENERGY.pemmican` or `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS` before touching 2yn demand levers. Run `bun run sim harvest --seed <tier>` to re-measure.

**Combat**
- `PLAYER_BASE_HP` ¬∑ `DMG_ARMOUR_MATRIX[dmgType][armourType]` ‚Äî read BOTH ways: damage multiplier vs the monster's hide class going out, mitigation divisor per armour piece coming in (`defense √∑ matrix`) ¬∑ `ARMOUR{pieceDefId} ‚Üí {armourType, defense}` ¬∑ `WEAPONS{defId} ‚Üí {dmgType, damage, tags}` ¬∑ `UNARMED_DAMAGE`
- **`MITIGATION_K` (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new ‚Äî 6)** ‚Äî the armour-power dial: incoming damage is now `dmgIn = max(CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN, dmg √ó MITIGATION_K/(MITIGATION_K + D))` where `D = Œ£(defense √∑ matrix)` (**supersedes flat subtraction**). Higher K = armour matters less (mitigation asymptotes toward 0% as K‚Üí‚àû); lower K = armour matters more. At today's K=6, full iron plate ‚âà ‚àí50%, mithril ‚âà ‚àí70% ‚Äî meaningful but never zeroed to chip, closing the M7 F1 hole where plate floored the entire bestiary
- `AFFINITIES[{monsterTag, itemTag}]` + `AFFINITY_MULTIPLIER` ‚Äî the hidden discoverable layer (silver‚Üîwerewolf, iron‚Üîfae, garlic‚Üîvampire); never shown by perception, **discovered post-fight** via `matchup.affinityFired` (D38)
- `POTION_HEAL` (default) ¬∑ `POTION_HEAL_BY{potionDefId}` (2026-07-04, `potion` 10 / `greater-potion` 20; absent = `POTION_HEAL`) ¬∑ `AUTO_POTION_THRESHOLD` (fraction of base HP) ‚Äî since si7.1 this also drives **auto-quaff inside engagement exchanges** (mirrors `autoEat`'s threshold-driven refill; toggled per-run by `autoQuaff`, default true) ¬∑ `CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN` ‚Äî the "HP always drains" floor, both directions (now also the mitigation formula's floor, see `MITIGATION_K` above)
- `QUAFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) ‚Äî energy to drink a potion OUTSIDE an engagement (82r "quaff anywhere"); in-combat quaff stays energy-free (its cost is tempo ‚Äî si7.1's combat balance was calibrated with it). Raise to make between-fights healing a real budget call; at ~a plains step it's a convenience price ¬∑ `DON_DOFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) ‚Äî energy to don/doff one piece of gear on the map (rejected while engaged). Raise to make pre-fight gear swaps a routing commitment instead of a free toggle
- **Ranged combat (D45, 2026-07-07):** `ARROW_STACK_CAP` (**20**) ‚Äî arrows per inventory slot; ammo is the ONE deep-stacking consumable (`stackCapOf` ‚Üí this cap; `consumableSlots` counts `ceil(units/cap)`), so a slot of arrows ‚âà 20 shots. Lower it to tighten the ammo-vs-food-vs-loot squeeze; raise it to cheapen bow logistics ¬∑ `ARROWS_PER_CRAFT` (**10**) ‚Äî arrows per craft batch (`pine-log + flint + feather` ‚Üí generous, per the user's "three different things to grab" call). These two together set shots-per-expedition-of-effort ‚Äî the bow style's real cost dial. `AMMO[]` is the catalog list (`slotOf` ‚Üí `"ammo"`). The opener (first exchange of a ranged engagement skips the retaliation) is a RULE, not a lever ‚Äî if it needs tuning, that's an evasion model (explicitly deferred). Arrows-out damage is `UNARMED_DAMAGE` (no new lever unless playtest wants one)
- `COMBAT_BUFF{battleItemDefId} ‚Üí {damageAdd?, mitigationAdd?}` (bzd, 2026-07-05; consumption reworked 90j/D50, 2026-07-09) ‚Äî packed battle items, added into the engagement's `damageAdd`/`mitigationAdd` (`elixir-of-power` +2 dmg, `warding-draught` +3 mitigation). **Used MANUALLY mid-fight** via the `use-item` action (90j) ‚Äî no longer auto-consumed at engage; unused items bank back. The buff persists for the rest of THAT engagement only. The T3-fight-only reward branch; they cost inventory slots (pqp) and let a fought-up player beat the Wyrm without full mithril (¬ß4.3). `BATTLE_ITEM[]` is the catalog list (`slotOf` ‚Üí `"battle-item"`). `resolveCombat` (atomic sim API) still models packed battle items as used at fight start, so sim/harness balance is numerically unchanged.
- `MONSTER_TIER_HP_CURVE` (si7.1: **8/16/28/54** by tier, steepened from the D34 48-HP boss baseline) / `MONSTER_TIER_DMG_CURVE` (si7.1: **4/8/14/24** by tier) ‚Äî read together with `MITIGATION_K` they set matchup-scaled toll bands at tier 1: a bare-kit good matchup costs ‚âà13% of `PLAYER_BASE_HP`, neutral ‚âà27%, bad ‚â•40% ‚Äî HP is a second run-budget, not a rounding error. Tier 4 (54 HP / 24 dmg) re-pins the D34 Wyrm gate: full mithril takes 9 strikes to kill it and eats 8 retaliations back, landing squarely on the 3-greater-potion threshold ¬∑ `MONSTERS{defId} ‚Üí {tier, dmgType, armourType, tags}` ¬∑ `LOOT_TABLE{monster}` (fixed drops, optional `chance?` for rare bonus drops like `dragonheart`@0.2 ‚Äî determinism needs no RNG, `rollLoot` is seed-keyed) ¬∑ `BIOMES{id}.creatureTable` ‚Äî **si7.1: now WEIGHTED** (`Record<defId, number>`, same shape as `materialTable`), replacing the old uniform pick. Tier-1/2 trash dominates the weight mass; each biome's tier-4 boss sits at the bottom (tundra's `ancient-wyrm` is 1 of a 16-weight table ‚Äî properly rare, not a uniform 1-in-6). Roster is now complete per biome (tier-1 melee/ranged/magic spread): woodland gained `shell-beetle` (melee/plate) and turned `forest-bandit` ranged; desert gained `mirage-wisp` (magic/robe); tundra gained `ice-crab` (melee/plate) ‚Äî each drops a part (`beetle-shell`/`wisp-essence`/`crab-shell`) feeding a new recipe (`plate-boots-beetle`/`fire-staff-wisp`/`ration-crab`). **m0a mid-tier additions (D48):** `giant-elk` (woodland, T2, melee/light, weight 3 ‚Äî drops `rich-venison √ó2`; no elk-antler; venison feeds `smoked-venison`); `dust-djinn` (desert, T2, magic/robe, weight 3 ‚Äî bow-bait; drops `djinn-ember √ó1`, the gateway to `elixir-of-power-thistle` and `elixir-of-power` without farming vampires); `frost-hatchling` (tundra, T2, magic/robe, weight 3 ‚Äî wyrm herald, `dragon` tag = wyrmbane affinity target; drops `hatchling-scale √ó1` always + `map-scroll √ó1` @15%; `scale-jerky` recipe renders scale into 2√ó rations)
- **Engagement model (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new)** ‚Äî combat is no longer atomic: `Expedition.combat?: Engagement` holds a live fight; `fight` runs ONE exchange per call (both sides swing once, not the whole bout); `flee` disengages at the cost of one parting hit taken **and resets the monster's HP** (no free disengage ‚Äî the monster is still there next time); `quaff` drinks a potion mid-engagement with no exchange spent. Non-combat actions (`move`/`gather`/`drop`/`drop-map`/`return`/`eat`) reject `engaged` while `combat` is set ‚Äî `flee` becomes the one always-available out (amends the D29 "`return` always legal" note, which no longer holds while engaged).
- `DETAIL_RADIUS` (2) + `VISION_RANGE_BONUS{toolDefId}` (spyglass +3) ‚Äî passive perception (9u9.2, D38): node KIND always visible; qualitative `detail` (species/material/tier/dmg+armour type ‚Äî **never the outcome**) resolves only within `DETAIL_RADIUS + Œ£ bonuses` of the player. Future glasses/cartography/scent items slot into `VISION_RANGE_BONUS`. (Replaced `SCOUT_ENERGY_COST`/`SCOUT_RADIUS`/`SCOUT_TOOL`.)

**Crafting**
- `RECIPE{itemDefId} ‚Üí {inputs, output}` ‚Äî the shared tree (M5, filled): tiered tools (`iron-pick` halves mining cost ‚Äî the "cheaper second run" demonstrator), backpacks, weapons incl. affinity gear (`silver-sword`), armour pieces, transport, food, potions. Cross-biome inputs are a **soft pull** (D27: silver best-farmed in tundra, obtainable anywhere), not a hard gate. `FOOD`/`POTION` catalogs list which defIds `pack`/`slotOf` accept in the consumable slots.

**Map & forecast** ‚Äî where prep skill lives
- `MAP_WIDTH` (20) / `MAP_HEIGHT` (60) ‚Äî the strip (e3j, replaces the old square `GRID_SIZE`): WIDTH is thumb-sized for phone portrait, HEIGHT is the long scrolling axis the reach economy hangs off (D42) ¬∑ `POI_DENSITY` (**60**, e3j ‚Äî was 18 on the old 20√ó20 map, ~3√ó the area) ¬∑ `POI_MIN_SPACING` ¬∑ `POI_PLACEMENT_ATTEMPTS` (2000, scaled up with density so seeded rejection-sampling still reliably fits every POI)
- `NOISE_FREQUENCY` ‚Äî Perlin sample step per tile; lower = larger, chunkier terrain regions
- `BARRIER_NOISE_FREQUENCY` (0.06, e3j) / `BARRIER_THRESHOLD` (0.68, e3j) ‚Äî the barrier layer (D42): a SECOND, lower-frequency noise field lays long ridges of `BIOMES[id].barrierTerrain` across the strip (the navigation puzzle); tiles whose barrier sample exceeds `BARRIER_THRESHOLD` become wall, then a connectivity carve guarantees every walkable tile stays one component (barriers are cost-walls a gating tool can open, never literal prisons). `BARRIER_NOISE_FREQUENCY` ‚â™ `NOISE_FREQUENCY` ‚Üí chunky ridges, not speckle; `BARRIER_THRESHOLD` is the "how walled is the world" dial (lower = more maze)
- `BIOMES{id} ‚Üí {terrainWeights, nodeTypeWeights, creatureTable, materialTable, barrierTerrain}` ‚Äî a biome is a **generation profile only** (D21): consumed by `generateGrid(mapSeed, biomeId)`, never consulted at runtime. Biomes shift likelihoods, not rules. `barrierTerrain` (e3j) is what a wall is made of in that biome (mountain in all three today). Start: woodland / desert / tundra; adding a biome = one data entry. (Subsumes the earlier flat `NOISE_THRESHOLDS` ‚Äî thresholds now live per-biome in `terrainWeights`.)
- POI positions sample walkable tiles only (walls carry no nodes) since e3j.
- **Held maps (D40, xzx, 2026-07-06):** `GameState.maps: MapItem[]` is the pocketed-map collection ‚Äî no lever this pass (`pocket-map` has **no cost and no cap**: "go nearby" means you're never gated, so hoarding buys nothing). If hoarding ever needs friction, add a `MAX_HELD_MAPS` cap lever here; `vintage` (runs-at-pocket) is flavour-only today but is the natural hook for a future map-decay lever.
- `CANDIDATE_MAP_COUNT` (3) ¬∑ `PREVIEW_FIDELITY` ‚Äî how much the map preview reveals beyond the biome-name headline (the master dial for how much preparation matters). Ships at **0** (biome-name headline only; `candidateMaps` returns empty `hints`); `town.previewHints` is structured so higher tiers ‚Äî and a later **cartography** system (craftable/editable maps) ‚Äî plug in without reshaping the preview.
- `MAP_DROP_CHANCE` (0.5, 8ec) ‚Äî humanoid-kill map-scroll drop rate (`CATEGORY_LOOT_TABLE.humanoid`). Regular but not guaranteed: humanoids reliably mean maps over a few kills without every kill printing one. Raise toward 1.0 if map supply feels starved; the carry-slot cost (each carried map = one slot for the run) is the real limiter.
- `EPITHETS` (q2k, D49) ‚Äî ordered notability table; a map whose generated content crosses a threshold gets its label appended to the display name ("‚Ä¶ of carbon"). Each entry's `test` is one of `{material, minCount}` / `{creatureTierAtLeast}` / `{nodeType, minShare}`; **priority = array order**, first match wins, most maps match nothing. This is the "how often is a map notable" dial ‚Äî **raise** a `minCount`/`minShare`/`creatureTierAtLeast` to make that epithet rarer (fewer named maps), **lower** it to name more maps; keep the whole sweep under ~40% named or epithets stop being notable (`test/epithet.test.ts` asserts this on a T1 offer sweep). Labels are qualitative only ‚Äî never encode a number (a perception guard, regex-asserted) ‚Äî and are the shared vocabulary cxq's ink affixes will reuse. Current: `the ancients` (T3+ creature ‚Äî the boss spawn-lottery tell), `gleaming` (mithril ‚â•2), `carbon` (coal ‚â•4), `the hunt` (monster ‚â•40% of POIs), `plenty` (herb ‚â•45%).

**Map tiers (2yn, D46, 2026-07-08)** ‚Äî difficulty/density/loot ramp applied by `tierProfile` at grid generation. T1 = identity (no transform). All levers below are in `src/data/constants.ts`.
- `MAP_TIER_MAX` (**5**) ‚Äî deepest tier; `min(sourceTier+1, MAP_TIER_MAX)` caps the drop-ladder. Raise to add more rungs; lower to compress progression.
- `POI_DENSITY_BY_TIER` ‚Äî POI count override per tier (absent tier uses `POI_DENSITY`). Current: T2 +2, T3 +4, T4 +6, T5 +8. Richer maps get denser nodes; raise the deltas to make high-tier maps feel significantly more rewarding, lower to make POI count a flat property of the strip.
- `MATERIAL_TIER_WEIGHT` ‚Äî per-material weight multiplier by map tier. Sparse: absent defId/tier = 1. Today: `coal` ramps to 4√ó at T4, `iron-ore` peaks at 2√ó T3‚Äì4, `mithril-ore` unlocks at T3 and reaches 3√ó at T5. This is the primary loot-quality dial per tier ‚Äî raise a weight to make that material more prevalent; setting a defId's T1 entry to anything other than 1 breaks the identity contract (asserted by test).
- `NODE_MAGNITUDE_WEIGHTS` ‚Äî distribution over yield classes {1,2,3} by tier. T1 = `{1:1}` (always base). Higher tiers shift mass toward class 3. Current: T5 weights are `{1:2, 2:4, 3:4}` ‚Äî about 40% chance of a 3√ó node. Raise the class-3 weight to make rich nodes more common; setting T1 to anything other than `{1:1}` breaks the identity contract.
- `NODE_MAGNITUDE_YIELD` ‚Äî yield multiplier per magnitude class: class 1 = 1√ó, class 2 = 2√ó, class 3 = 3√ó. These multiply `GATHER_YIELD[kind]`; adjust them to change how dramatic the rich-node bonus feels (flatten to make magnitude a flavour label, steepen to make T5 rich nodes the dominant supply source).
- `MAP_TIER_CREATURE_ADD` ‚Äî **biome-scoped** boss gate (the SINGLE source of boss spawns): `Record<BiomeId, Record<tier, Record<defId, weight>>>`. Each entry is the FULL weight-add for that biome+tier (not a delta). Bosses are removed from the base `BIOMES[*].creatureTable` and live exclusively here ‚Äî `tierProfile` merges this layer on top. Current gates: desert `dust-vampire` from T2 (weight 1‚Üí2‚Üí2‚Üí3 at T2‚Äì5); tundra `ice-troll` from T2, `ancient-wyrm` from T3 (graduated miniboss‚Üíwyrm arc, each weight ramps with tier). Adding a boss = add its defId to its native biome's entries here (never to `creatureTable`). Raise a weight to make a boss more common within the tier; add it at a lower tier to gate it earlier.
- `TERRAIN_WEIGHT_TIER_SHIFT` ‚Äî per-terrain weight multiplier by tier. Absent tier/terrain = 1. Current: mountain and river get progressively heavier from T2‚ÜíT5 (1.15‚Üí1.7), ice follows from T3. This is the energy-cost ramp: higher weights make obstacle terrain more prevalent, forcing longer routes and more food.
