# Balance Levers

The POC ships with *feel-pass* values, not balanced ones. The discipline that keeps the long game open: **every tunable is a named, documented constant in `src/data/`, grouped by system, commented with what pulling it does.** Engine logic never hardcodes a number — it reads a lever. Tuning = turning labelled dials, not hunting for stray `7`s.

## Lever groups

**Energy economy** — how far/long a trip lasts. Stamina model (D41/dtv): energy is CURRENT stamina on a max/current bar; you embark at `MAX_ENERGY` and EAT food to refill toward max. Total reach ≈ `MAX_ENERGY + Σ(food restore × tentMult)`.
- `MAX_ENERGY` (300, 2026-07-06 dtv) — the stamina ceiling; `expedition.energy` starts here on embark regardless of packed food, and `expedition.maxEnergy` = this + any gear bonus (see `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS`). Bumping it raises baseline reach; a difficulty re-tune for the extra reach is a playtest follow-up
- `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS{toolDefId}` (si7.2, 2026-07-08, new — `canteen: 100`) — extra maxEnergy granted by equipping the named tool; `energyCapOf(equipment)` sums these at embark. The `canteen` (recipe: `copper-ore×2 + deer-hide×1`) is the first capacity-gear item, raising the ceiling to 400 (`MAX_ENERGY + 100`). Raise the bonus to make the canteen a stronger progression lever; add more entries for higher-tier capacity gear (m0a scope). `canteen` is catalogued under `TOOL_CAPABILITY ("provision")` and `TOOL_QUALITY (1)` so it packs as a tool slot.
- `TENT_FOOD_MULTIPLIER` (1.5, 2026-07-06 dtv) — a `tent` (durable "camp" tool) multiplies energy restored per food unit, so each ration goes 50% further. Tunable
- `ENERGY_PER_FOOD` — default energy RESTORED per food unit eaten (fallback for `FOOD_ENERGY`)
- `FOOD_ENERGY{foodDefId}` (`ration` 80 / `trail-ration` **130** / `berries` 30 / `jam` 120 / `pemmican` **240** / `apple` **40** / `smoked-venison` **200** / `blubber-stew` **160**) — RESTORE per food unit eaten (D41: was embark energy, now refill-per-unit). Three-rung food ladder (si7.2, D47): `ration` is the floor (do NOT drop below 80 — risks the forage-only tundra sustainability floor); `trail-ration` compressed 160→**130** (compressed toward the ration floor; pemmican now occupies the upper tier); `pemmican` (**240**, recipe: `drake-hide×1 + stale-berries×2`) is the ONE proof tier-food — higher-tier packs spend fewer slots on the same energy, making progression tangible. `eatToRefill` is waste-free (never overfills past max). **Auto-eat rule (mco, D61 — supersedes D48's least-dense-first):** auto-eat is **OFF by default** and **scoped to one DESIGNATED food** (`Expedition.autoEatFood`, a food defId; set via `set-auto-eat-food`, right-click a food unit on web). `eatToRefill(food, energy, max, targetDefId, tentMult?)` eats ONLY units of the designated food, waste-free; nothing auto-eats until you designate. The old cross-food least-dense-first selection is retired (scoping to one food removes the ordering problem). **Manual `eat` (m0a, D48, unchanged):** targets the MOST-dense unit (the reserve auto-eat leaves alone) and jumps energy TO `foodEnergy × tentMult`, which may exceed `maxEnergy` (over-full drains normally); web bar surfaces the surplus as `+N`. No tent-safe cap on food density. Absent = `ENERGY_PER_FOOD`. `berries`/`jam` (e3j) are the fresh/processed pair: `berries` is weak-but-immediate fresh forage (eat on the trail or lose it to staleness); `jam` is the processed payoff (1.5 rations/slot) — see `FRESH_TO_STALE` below. **m0a fresh-food additions:** `apple` (40, woodland orchard `wood` nodes — material defId = food defId, stales to `bruised-apple` at banking via `FRESH_TO_STALE`; `apple-jam` recipe: `bruised-apple ×3 → jam`); `smoked-venison` (200, `rich-venison + salt` — cross-biome elk×desert pull, the manual-over-eat reserve); `blubber-stew` (160, `seal-blubber + ice-moss` — tundra tier-food)
- **Fresh→processed food (D42, e3j):** `FRESH_TO_STALE{freshDefId → staleDefId}` (`berries → stale-berries`) maps a fresh forage defId to the material it becomes if hauled home uneaten — `endExpedition` (banking) converts any leftover fresh units at return, so eating fresh forage on-map is good NOW, while hauling it home stales it into a craftable input (`jam`'s recipe: `stale-berries ×3 → jam`). Stale forms are materials, not food (`slotOf` never returns `"food"` for them), so they can't be packed back out for another eat-fresh loop — the rule enforces itself with no extra check. This is the live "eat-now-or-haul-home" call the reach economy needs food to be.
- (retired) `BASE_ENERGY_FLOOR` — removed by D41; its "recoverable short run" role is now "embark at `MAX_ENERGY`, just nothing to refill with"
- `TERRAIN_COST{plains 10, mud 15, ice 20, river 30, mountain ∞}` — **absolute** step energy on a ×10 scale (svz, graded movement); `Infinity` = the one hard gate (mountain), enabled by `climbing-pick`
- `MIN_STEP` (5) — floor: a discounted step never costs less than this
- `TERRAIN_GATE{terrain → toolDefId → {enable?, discount?}}` — gear modifiers: `enable` turns an impassable terrain finite (climbing-pick/mountain → 40); `discount` subtracts points (raft/river −20, waders/mud −5, ice-cleats/ice −15 → glide). Each tool = one slot
- `TRANSPORT_MULTIPLIER{transport → terrain → divisor}` — **per-terrain** move-cost divisor (svz): horse fast on open ground (plains ÷2), wagon the ice answer (ice ÷2), mule slow-but-hauler (÷0.8); absent terrain / on-foot = ÷1

**Carry** — loot vs supplies tension (UNIT-based since Phase 2 / pqp)
- `BASE_CARRY_SLOTS` — inventory slots with NO backpack (**6** since pqp) · `BACKPACK_SLOTS{tier}` — total slots per backpack (replaces the base): `starter` 8 / `leather` 12 / `large-pack` 16 · `STACK_CAP` — max qty per **loot** stack (**5**); consumables/tools do NOT stack
- **pqp (2026-07-05, supersedes D23):** each food/potion/battleItem **unit** and **each tool** is one slot; only loot stacks. Food is eaten just-in-time (`food.digest`) and its slot frees mid-run; **uneaten food banks back** on return. So the squeeze is *temporal* — cramped early (heavy food), roomy late — and packing a supply is a live, visible loot slot given up. Caps were bumped (3→6, 4/6/8→8/12/16) for the ~5× consumable pressure vs the old per-stack model. Re-tuning: keep the sustainability harness green (`test/harness-sustainability.test.ts`).
- **Carry sources stack (zhn, 2026-07-05):** `carryCap(equipment)` = backpack tier + `TRANSPORT_CARRY{transport}` (a beast/cart bonus: horse 2, mule 4, wagon 6) + `PANNIERS_SLOTS{panniers}` (4) — panniers only count with a `BEAST_TRANSPORTS` (horse/mule), so a mule+panniers is a hauler and a wagon can't wear them. `panniers` is a new equipment slot (worn/free, durable, banks back).

**Gathering**
- `NODE_HARDNESS{nodeType}` — energy cost numerator · `TOOL_QUALITY{toolDefId}` — cost divisor **AND tier** (D31): quality doubles as the max material tier the tool can work · `GATHER_YIELD{nodeType}` — qty per (one-shot) node
- `NODE_TOOL{nodeType}` — required capability (herb = bare hands) · `TOOL_CAPABILITY{toolDefId}` — tiered tools are data-only (D21: all per node type, never per biome)
- `MATERIAL_TIER{materialDefId}` (D31, 2026-07-04) — a material's tier; gather rejects `tool-too-weak` when the best held tool's quality < this. Absent = tier 1. This **one lever gates the whole tech tree** (no smelting step): coal/silver (T2) need an iron-pick, mithril (T3) needs a steel-pick. The visible-but-locked node is the pull up the ladder.
- Yield defIds come from `BIOMES{id}.materialTable`, stamped onto POIs at generation (D25) — materials across 3 biomes feed one shared recipe tree (M5); tiered materials (coal/ironwood/drake-hide T2, mithril T3) are data additions

**Harvest sim + sustainability targets (si7.2, D47, 2026-07-08)** — calibrated reference for food-economy tuning. `src/sim/harvest.ts` runs a monster-aware greedy walker (routes around live monsters via Dijkstra; never engages) at a given map tier and reports the fraction of gatherable POIs cleared per seed. Used to set and guard the food-economy targets.
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_TIER_TARGET` (**0.50**) — fraction of gatherable POIs a reference walker clears on a tier-N map (measured with a pemmican loadout). Calibration rationale: 60% is the design aspiration; the no-optimal-router reference walker lands at ~50% — a conservative floor; real players route better. Do NOT lower below 0.40 (tier food must buy real reach).
- `HARVEST_FRACTION_BASE_TARGET` (**0.25**) — same walker at the base tier (tier 1). Set to ~half the tier target; the 2× ratio is the invariant that proves tiered food earns slot efficiency, not just raw restore. Measured: tier=50%, base=25%, ratio=1.99 — gate passed.
- Hard gate: `tier_fraction ≥ 1.8 × base_fraction`. If a food/energy tuning pass breaks this, revisit `FOOD_ENERGY.pemmican` or `ENERGY_CAP_BONUS` before touching 2yn demand levers. Run `bun run sim harvest --seed <tier>` to re-measure.

**Combat**
- `PLAYER_BASE_HP` · `DMG_ARMOUR_MATRIX[dmgType][armourType]` — read BOTH ways: damage multiplier vs the monster's hide class going out, mitigation divisor per armour piece coming in (`defense ÷ matrix`) · `ARMOUR{pieceDefId} → {armourType, defense}` · `WEAPONS{defId} → {dmgType, damage, tags}` · `UNARMED_DAMAGE`
- **`MITIGATION_K` (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new — 6)** — the armour-power dial: incoming damage is now `dmgIn = max(CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN, dmg × MITIGATION_K/(MITIGATION_K + D))` where `D = Σ(defense ÷ matrix)` (**supersedes flat subtraction**). Higher K = armour matters less (mitigation asymptotes toward 0% as K→∞); lower K = armour matters more. At today's K=6, full iron plate ≈ −50%, mithril ≈ −70% — meaningful but never zeroed to chip, closing the M7 F1 hole where plate floored the entire bestiary
- `AFFINITIES[{monsterTag, itemTag}]` + `AFFINITY_MULTIPLIER` — the hidden discoverable layer (silver↔werewolf, iron↔fae, garlic↔vampire); never shown by perception, **discovered post-fight** via `matchup.affinityFired` (D38)
- `POTION_HEAL` (default) · `POTION_HEAL_BY{potionDefId}` (2026-07-04, `potion` 10 / `greater-potion` 20; absent = `POTION_HEAL`) · `AUTO_POTION_THRESHOLD` (fraction of base HP) — since si7.1 this also drives **auto-quaff inside engagement exchanges** (mirrors `autoEat`'s threshold-driven refill; toggled per-run by `autoQuaff`, default true) · `CHIP_DAMAGE_MIN` — the "HP always drains" floor, both directions (now also the mitigation formula's floor, see `MITIGATION_K` above)
- `QUAFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) — energy to drink a potion OUTSIDE an engagement (82r "quaff anywhere"); in-combat quaff stays energy-free (its cost is tempo — si7.1's combat balance was calibrated with it). Raise to make between-fights healing a real budget call; at ~a plains step it's a convenience price · `DON_DOFF_ENERGY` (2, 2026-07-07 D44) — energy to don/doff one piece of gear on the map (rejected while engaged). Raise to make pre-fight gear swaps a routing commitment instead of a free toggle
- **Ranged combat (D45, 2026-07-07):** `ARROW_STACK_CAP` (**20**) — arrows per inventory slot; ammo is the ONE deep-stacking consumable (`stackCapOf` → this cap; `consumableSlots` counts `ceil(units/cap)`), so a slot of arrows ≈ 20 shots. Lower it to tighten the ammo-vs-food-vs-loot squeeze; raise it to cheapen bow logistics · `ARROWS_PER_CRAFT` (**10**) — arrows per craft batch (`pine-log + flint + feather` → generous, per the user's "three different things to grab" call). These two together set shots-per-expedition-of-effort — the bow style's real cost dial. `AMMO[]` is the catalog list (`slotOf` → `"ammo"`). The opener (first exchange of a ranged engagement skips the retaliation) is a RULE, not a lever — if it needs tuning, that's an evasion model (explicitly deferred). Arrows-out damage is `UNARMED_DAMAGE` (no new lever unless playtest wants one) · **Fletching yield-mod (ke3.3/D54, 2026-07-09):** `ARROW_SHAFTS_PER_LOG` (**3**) — the `arrow-shaft` recipe's `outputScale.qtyPer`; shafts per oak-log = this × the fletch tool's `TOOL_QUALITY` (`fletchers-knife` q1 → 3, `steel-fletchers-knife` q2 → 6). `arrows-fletched` consumes `ARROW_SHAFTS_PER_LOG` shafts + flint + feather → `ARROWS_PER_CRAFT` arrows, so at the base knife one log = one batch (parallel to the direct `arrows` recipe — no free lunch), and the steel knife HALVES wood/arrow (the visible tool payoff, repays 57l). Raise `ARROW_SHAFTS_PER_LOG` to make the whole fletched line cheaper; the knife tiers in `TOOL_QUALITY` set the payoff slope

- **Recipe gates & yield-mod mechanism (ke3, crafting-depth):** not tuning dials but the grammar levers hang off — `RECIPE[].requires{station?,tools?,terrain?}` (presence gates; tools = AND-semantics defIds via `TOOL_CAPABILITY`), `RECIPE[].field?` (expedition-craftable), `RECIPE[].buildsStation?` (routes output into `state.stations`, non-bank), `RECIPE[].outputScale{capability,qtyPer}` (tool-quality-scaled yield). All absent on existing recipes → economy unchanged. `StationId` = `smokehouse|alchemical-desk|anvil`. Station CONTENT lands with the ke3.5+ proof slices
- **Field crafting (ke3.4/D55, 2026-07-09):** `FIELD_CRAFT_ENERGY` (**10**) — flat energy to field-craft a `field:true` recipe on expedition, spent before the craft then waste-free auto-eat (gather pattern). Half a herb gather (20) — a deliberate-stop cost that trades against reach, not free. Raise to make field crafting a heavier commitment; the field tool pool is carried-only (`equipped ∪ carry`), so packing the `fire-kit` (heat gate, quality 1) is the loadout price of cooking out there. Content: `cooked-venison` (`FOOD_ENERGY` **150** — denser than a ration, below the home-smoked 200) from `rich-venison + oak-log`. Food outputs append to the food BACK (auto-eat burns them last); `requires.terrain` gates on the current tile or a 4-neighbour
- **The forge (ke3.7/D58, 2026-07-09):** all 15 metal `plate-*` recipes (iron/steel/mithril) gated behind `requires:{station:"anvil", tools:["blacksmiths-hammer"]}`. `anvil` station (`iron-ore×3 + oak-log×2`), `blacksmiths-hammer` tool (capability "smith", `iron-ore×2`). No new armour, no combat-math change — a one-time progression gate on heavy armour (the chitin/carapace plate alternates stay ungated). If plate feels too gated in playtest, loosen by ungating the base `plate-*` set or cheapening the anvil here
- **Alchemy content (ke3.6/D57, 2026-07-09):** `draught` (`POTION_HEAL_BY` **8** — the basic heal, brewable in the FIELD from a river-filled vial or at home; the only mid-run-craftable potion), `greater-draught` (`POTION_HEAL_BY` **20** = greater-potion — the alchemical-desk strong heal the field kit can't make, a parallel not a power-creep). `glassware` = the alchemy kit-tool (capability "alchemy", quality 1). Materials: `glass-vial` (town `flint×2`), `water-vial` (field-only, filled at a river). `alchemical-desk` station (`glass-vial×3 + iron-ore×2`). Tune draught heals here; `field-draught` uses `FIELD_CRAFT_ENERGY` like every field craft. Field-crafted POTION outputs land in `loadout.potions` (quaffable immediately)
- **Cooking loop content (ke3.5/D56, 2026-07-09):** the proof-slice foods — `cooked-berries` (`FOOD_ENERGY` **100**, `fire-kit`, `berries×2 + oak-log`; universal-forage cook), `stew` (`FOOD_ENERGY` **220**, needs `fire-kit`+`cooking-pot`, `rich-venison + berries×2 + oak-log`; premium field food, under the pemmican 240 reserve). `cooking-pot` = a second cooking tool (capability "simmer", quality 1, town recipe `iron-ore×2`). `smokehouse` station (`oak-log×3 + iron-ore×2`) now gates the existing `smoked-venison` (200). **Invariant (`cooking-loop.test`):** every `field:true` FOOD recipe must clear `Σ FOOD_ENERGY[food inputs] + FIELD_CRAFT_ENERGY` — cooking always nets positive stamina, so raising `FIELD_CRAFT_ENERGY` or lowering a cooked-food value past that bar fails the suite. Tune cooked densities here, not in the recipe
- `COMBAT_BUFF{battleItemDefId} → {damageAdd?, mitigationAdd?}` (bzd, 2026-07-05; consumption reworked 90j/D50, 2026-07-09) — packed battle items, added into the engagement's `damageAdd`/`mitigationAdd` (`elixir-of-power` +2 dmg, `warding-draught` +3 mitigation). **Used MANUALLY mid-fight** via the `use-item` action (90j) — no longer auto-consumed at engage; unused items bank back. The buff persists for the rest of THAT engagement only. The T3-fight-only reward branch; they cost inventory slots (pqp) and let a fought-up player beat the Wyrm without full mithril (§4.3). `BATTLE_ITEM[]` is the catalog list (`slotOf` → `"battle-item"`). `resolveCombat` (atomic sim API) still models packed battle items as used at fight start, so sim/harness balance is numerically unchanged.
- `WEAPON_ENHANCEMENT{enhancementDefId} → {charges, flatDamage?, affinityTag?, poison?{dmg,rounds}}` (D60, 2026-07-09) — the mid-run weapon buff/coating lever. `charges` = player strikes the coating survives before clearing; `flatDamage` = +N to `playerDamage` per strike (whetstone); `affinityTag` = a MONSTER tag the coating matches DIRECTLY for ×`AFFINITY_MULTIPLIER` (silver-oil→werewolf, drake-oil→dragon — matched-or-not, never stacks past ×2, so it can't double-dip a weapon that already matches); `poison{dmg,rounds}` = a DoT the monster suffers each round end, independent of charges (venom-oil). **Feel-pass starting values (tune in playtest):** whetstone `{charges:6, flatDamage:2}`, silver-oil `{charges:5, affinityTag:"werewolf"}`, drake-oil `{charges:5, affinityTag:"dragon"}`, venom-oil `{charges:5, poison:{dmg:3, rounds:4}}`. `ENHANCEMENT[]` is the catalog list (`slotOf`→`"enhancement"`; ⇔ the table, asserted). Applied via the `enhance` action (engaged or not, no exchange/energy); rides `Expedition.weaponBuff`, applying REPLACES it (old charges lost). Purely additive opt-in offense: absent = every combat number byte-identical (the balance harness passes un-edited; `resolveCombat` models the coating strike-by-strike identically to the interactive fight). Recipes: oils on the `still` station (`glass-vial×3 + copper-ore×2` to build), whetstone at the anvil forge. **These are the G1 dial** — if a single sword still feels inert, raise the affinity oils' reach or the whetstone flat; if enhancement trivialises a tier, cut charges.
- `MONSTER_TIER_HP_CURVE` (si7.1: **8/16/28/54** by tier, steepened from the D34 48-HP boss baseline) / `MONSTER_TIER_DMG_CURVE` (si7.1: **4/8/14/24** by tier) — read together with `MITIGATION_K` they set matchup-scaled toll bands at tier 1: a bare-kit good matchup costs ≈13% of `PLAYER_BASE_HP`, neutral ≈27%, bad ≥40% — HP is a second run-budget, not a rounding error. Tier 4 (54 HP / 24 dmg) re-pins the D34 Wyrm gate: full mithril takes 9 strikes to kill it and eats 8 retaliations back, landing squarely on the 3-greater-potion threshold · `MONSTERS{defId} → {tier, dmgType, armourType, tags}` · `LOOT_TABLE{monster}` (fixed drops, optional `chance?` for rare bonus drops like `dragonheart`@0.2 — determinism needs no RNG, `rollLoot` is seed-keyed) · `BIOMES{id}.creatureTable` — **si7.1: now WEIGHTED** (`Record<defId, number>`, same shape as `materialTable`), replacing the old uniform pick. Tier-1/2 trash dominates the weight mass; each biome's tier-4 boss sits at the bottom (tundra's `ancient-wyrm` is 1 of a 16-weight table — properly rare, not a uniform 1-in-6). Roster is now complete per biome (tier-1 melee/ranged/magic spread): woodland gained `shell-beetle` (melee/plate) and turned `forest-bandit` ranged; desert gained `mirage-wisp` (magic/robe); tundra gained `ice-crab` (melee/plate) — each drops a part (`beetle-shell`/`wisp-essence`/`crab-shell`) feeding a new recipe (`plate-boots-beetle`/`fire-staff-wisp`/`ration-crab`). **m0a mid-tier additions (D48):** `giant-elk` (woodland, T2, melee/light, weight 3 — drops `rich-venison ×2`; no elk-antler; venison feeds `smoked-venison`); `dust-djinn` (desert, T2, magic/robe, weight 3 — bow-bait; drops `djinn-ember ×1`, the gateway to `elixir-of-power-thistle` and `elixir-of-power` without farming vampires); `frost-hatchling` (tundra, T2, magic/robe, weight 3 — wyrm herald, `dragon` tag = wyrmbane affinity target; drops `hatchling-scale ×1` always + `map-scroll ×1` @15%; `scale-jerky` recipe renders scale into 2× rations)
- **Engagement model (si7.1, 2026-07-06, new)** — combat is no longer atomic: `Expedition.combat?: Engagement` holds a live fight; `fight` runs ONE exchange per call (both sides swing once, not the whole bout); `flee` disengages at the cost of one parting hit taken **and resets the monster's HP** (no free disengage — the monster is still there next time); `quaff` drinks a potion mid-engagement with no exchange spent. Non-combat actions (`move`/`gather`/`drop`/`drop-map`/`return`/`eat`) reject `engaged` while `combat` is set — `flee` becomes the one always-available out (amends the D29 "`return` always legal" note, which no longer holds while engaged).
- `DETAIL_RADIUS` (2) + `VISION_RANGE_BONUS{toolDefId}` (spyglass +3) — passive perception (9u9.2, D38): node KIND always visible; qualitative `detail` (species/material/tier/dmg+armour type — **never the outcome**) resolves only within `DETAIL_RADIUS + Σ bonuses` of the player. Future glasses/cartography/scent items slot into `VISION_RANGE_BONUS`. (Replaced `SCOUT_ENERGY_COST`/`SCOUT_RADIUS`/`SCOUT_TOOL`.)
- `SURVEY_ENERGY` (5, 54f/D51) — energy to actively **survey** one POI at range with a vision-capability tool (`TOOL_CAPABILITY[t]==='vision'`), resolving its `detail` whole-map away and recording it in `Expedition.surveyed` (perceive then treats it as always-in-radius; it persists for the run). The spyglass's ACTIVE verb — the price of studying the glass — set at ~half a plains step. Raise to make each survey a real budget call, lower toward free to make a vision tool a pervasive detail-reveal. Range is deliberately the whole map; the energy + slot + not-engaged gate is the cost (revisit if a playtest shows abuse).
- `TOOL_PURPOSE{capability}` (D59, 2026-07-09, gate-legibility) — **display-only, not a balance dial:** maps a tool CAPABILITY to a short "what door it opens" clause (heat→"enables field cooking", alchemy→"enables field brewing", smith→"forges metal plate at an anvil", vision→"reveals a far node's material tier when you survey it"). Surfaced in item tooltips (`describe`) + the web/console field-craft affordance so a kit-tool stops being an unmarked key (3/3 playtesters never found field crafting). No mechanic reads it; a capability absent here (movement/gather tools) simply gets no clause. Add an entry when a new capability wants its purpose signposted.

**Crafting**
- `RECIPE{itemDefId} → {inputs, output}` — the shared tree (M5, filled): tiered tools (`iron-pick` halves mining cost — the "cheaper second run" demonstrator), backpacks, weapons incl. affinity gear (`silver-sword`), armour pieces, transport, food, potions. Cross-biome inputs are a **soft pull** (D27: silver best-farmed in tundra, obtainable anywhere), not a hard gate. `FOOD`/`POTION` catalogs list which defIds `pack`/`slotOf` accept in the consumable slots.

**Map & forecast** — where prep skill lives
- `MAP_WIDTH` (20) / `MAP_HEIGHT` (60) — the strip (e3j, replaces the old square `GRID_SIZE`): WIDTH is thumb-sized for phone portrait, HEIGHT is the long scrolling axis the reach economy hangs off (D42) · `POI_DENSITY` (**60**, e3j — was 18 on the old 20×20 map, ~3× the area) · `POI_MIN_SPACING` · `POI_PLACEMENT_ATTEMPTS` (2000, scaled up with density so seeded rejection-sampling still reliably fits every POI)
- `NOISE_FREQUENCY` — Perlin sample step per tile; lower = larger, chunkier terrain regions
- `BARRIER_NOISE_FREQUENCY` (0.06, e3j) / `BARRIER_THRESHOLD` (0.68, e3j) — the barrier layer (D42): a SECOND, lower-frequency noise field lays long ridges of `BIOMES[id].barrierTerrain` across the strip (the navigation puzzle); tiles whose barrier sample exceeds `BARRIER_THRESHOLD` become wall, then a connectivity carve guarantees every walkable tile stays one component (barriers are cost-walls a gating tool can open, never literal prisons). `BARRIER_NOISE_FREQUENCY` ≪ `NOISE_FREQUENCY` → chunky ridges, not speckle; `BARRIER_THRESHOLD` is the "how walled is the world" dial (lower = more maze)
- `BIOMES{id} → {terrainWeights, nodeTypeWeights, creatureTable, materialTable, barrierTerrain}` — a biome is a **generation profile only** (D21): consumed by `generateGrid(mapSeed, biomeId)`, never consulted at runtime. Biomes shift likelihoods, not rules. `barrierTerrain` (e3j) is what a wall is made of in that biome (mountain in all three today). Start: woodland / desert / tundra; adding a biome = one data entry. (Subsumes the earlier flat `NOISE_THRESHOLDS` — thresholds now live per-biome in `terrainWeights`.)
- POI positions sample walkable tiles only (walls carry no nodes) since e3j.
- **Held maps (D40, xzx, 2026-07-06):** `GameState.maps: MapItem[]` is the pocketed-map collection — no lever this pass (`pocket-map` has **no cost and no cap**: "go nearby" means you're never gated, so hoarding buys nothing). If hoarding ever needs friction, add a `MAX_HELD_MAPS` cap lever here; `vintage` (runs-at-pocket) is flavour-only today but is the natural hook for a future map-decay lever.
- `CANDIDATE_MAP_COUNT` (3) · `PREVIEW_FIDELITY` — how much the map preview reveals beyond the biome-name headline (the master dial for how much preparation matters). Ships at **0** (biome-name headline only; `candidateMaps` returns empty `hints`); `town.previewHints` is structured so higher tiers — and a later **cartography** system (craftable/editable maps) — plug in without reshaping the preview.
- `MAP_DROP_CHANCE` (0.5, 8ec) — humanoid-kill map-scroll drop rate (`CATEGORY_LOOT_TABLE.humanoid`). Regular but not guaranteed: humanoids reliably mean maps over a few kills without every kill printing one. Raise toward 1.0 if map supply feels starved; the carry-slot cost (each carried map = one slot for the run) is the real limiter.
- `EPITHETS` (q2k, D49) — ordered notability table; a map whose generated content crosses a threshold gets its label appended to the display name ("… of carbon"). Each entry's `test` is one of `{material, minCount}` / `{creatureTierAtLeast}` / `{nodeType, minShare}`; **priority = array order**, first match wins, most maps match nothing. This is the "how often is a map notable" dial — **raise** a `minCount`/`minShare`/`creatureTierAtLeast` to make that epithet rarer (fewer named maps), **lower** it to name more maps; keep the whole sweep under ~40% named or epithets stop being notable (`test/epithet.test.ts` asserts this on a T1 offer sweep). Labels are qualitative only — never encode a number (a perception guard, regex-asserted) — and are the shared vocabulary cxq's ink affixes will reuse. Current: `the ancients` (T3+ creature — the boss spawn-lottery tell), `gleaming` (mithril ≥2), `carbon` (coal ≥4), `the hunt` (monster ≥40% of POIs), `plenty` (herb ≥45%).
- `INKS` + `AFFIX_EFFECTS` (cxq, D52) — cartography: `INKS{inkDefId} → {domain, pool}` are craftable bank materials (`ore-ink` ← copper×2 + potion — a copper sink; `herb-ink` ← forest-herb×2 + potion); the `ink` action consumes one and rolls an affix from `pool` onto a held map, REPLACING any existing affix of the same `domain`. `AFFIX_EFFECTS{affixId} → {label, materialWeightMul?, nodeTypeWeightMul?}` are the generation biases, applied MULTIPLICATIVELY after `tierProfile` in `generateGrid` (a `materialWeightMul` only bites where the defId already exists in the biome). Labels REUSE q2k's `EPITHETS` vocabulary (`carbon`, `gleaming`, …) — read the language in offers, write it with inks. This is the farm-economy nuance dial: **raise** a `materialWeightMul` to make inking a stronger pull toward that resource, **widen** a `pool` to make the roll less predictable (more chase), **add** an ink/domain for a new farm axis. Base (un-inked) maps are byte-identical — affixes are the identity-at-absent modifier, they never shift the RNG stream.

**Map tiers (2yn, D46, 2026-07-08)** — difficulty/density/loot ramp applied by `tierProfile` at grid generation. T1 = identity (no transform). All levers below are in `src/data/constants.ts`.
- `MAP_TIER_MAX` (**5**) — deepest tier; `min(sourceTier+1, MAP_TIER_MAX)` caps the drop-ladder. Raise to add more rungs; lower to compress progression.
- `POI_DENSITY_BY_TIER` — POI count override per tier (absent tier uses `POI_DENSITY`). Current: T2 +2, T3 +4, T4 +6, T5 +8. Richer maps get denser nodes; raise the deltas to make high-tier maps feel significantly more rewarding, lower to make POI count a flat property of the strip.
- `MATERIAL_TIER_WEIGHT` — per-material weight multiplier by map tier. Sparse: absent defId/tier = 1. Today: `coal` ramps to 4× at T4, `iron-ore` peaks at 2× T3–4, `mithril-ore` unlocks at T3 and reaches 3× at T5. This is the primary loot-quality dial per tier — raise a weight to make that material more prevalent; setting a defId's T1 entry to anything other than 1 breaks the identity contract (asserted by test).
- `NODE_MAGNITUDE_WEIGHTS` — distribution over yield classes {1,2,3} by tier. T1 = `{1:1}` (always base). Higher tiers shift mass toward class 3. Current: T5 weights are `{1:2, 2:4, 3:4}` — about 40% chance of a 3× node. Raise the class-3 weight to make rich nodes more common; setting T1 to anything other than `{1:1}` breaks the identity contract.
- `NODE_MAGNITUDE_YIELD` — yield multiplier per magnitude class: class 1 = 1×, class 2 = 2×, class 3 = 3×. These multiply `GATHER_YIELD[kind]`; adjust them to change how dramatic the rich-node bonus feels (flatten to make magnitude a flavour label, steepen to make T5 rich nodes the dominant supply source).
- `MAP_TIER_CREATURE_ADD` — **biome-scoped** boss gate (the SINGLE source of boss spawns): `Record<BiomeId, Record<tier, Record<defId, weight>>>`. Each entry is the FULL weight-add for that biome+tier (not a delta). Bosses are removed from the base `BIOMES[*].creatureTable` and live exclusively here — `tierProfile` merges this layer on top. Current gates: desert `dust-vampire` from T2 (weight 1→2→2→3 at T2–5); tundra `ice-troll` from T2, `ancient-wyrm` from T3 (graduated miniboss→wyrm arc, each weight ramps with tier). Adding a boss = add its defId to its native biome's entries here (never to `creatureTable`). Raise a weight to make a boss more common within the tier; add it at a lower tier to gate it earlier.
- `TERRAIN_WEIGHT_TIER_SHIFT` — per-terrain weight multiplier by tier. Absent tier/terrain = 1. Current: mountain and river get progressively heavier from T2→T5 (1.15→1.7), ice follows from T3. This is the energy-cost ramp: higher weights make obstacle terrain more prevalent, forcing longer routes and more food. Raise shifts to make high-tier maps punishing to navigate; lower to let the creature/loot ramp carry difficulty without geography.

**Return flavor (xwp, D62, 2026-07-10)** — cosmetic beat shown on a VOLUNTARY return only (never on defeat's soft-fail). Pure flavor, no mechanic change; free return still stands. Bucket picked in `engine/flavor.ts` from `(energy, mapTier, leftover-cooked-food)`, variant chosen by a seeded `rand`. All levers in `src/data/constants.ts`.
- `RETURN_FRESH_FRACTION` (**0.5**) — energy > this × maxEnergy is a "fresh" return (bored/beneath); at-or-below is "weary"; exactly 0 is "spent". Raise to make more returns read as tired; lower to make the disdain/boredom beats fire more readily.
- `RETURN_TIER_HIGH` (**3**) — a *spent* (energy 0) return at mapTier ≥ this uses the "long journey home" pool; at `MAP_TIER_MAX` it uses the "absurdly long trek" epic pool; below it, the plain "trudge home" pool. Purely which exhausted-copy set plays — no mechanical effect.
- `RETURN_COOKED_FOODS` — cooked/prepared keeper foods (`cooked-venison`, `cooked-berries`, `stew`, `smoked-venison`, `blubber-stew`); leftover qty>0 of any + a fresh return triggers the "beneath you" snark instead of the plain "bored" pool. Add a food here to make leaving with it read as disdain.
- `RETURN_FLAVOR` — the copy pools themselves, one array per bucket (`spent-low`/`spent-high`/`spent-epic`/`weary`/`bored`/`beneath`), 3 variants each. Edit freely; a test asserts no em dashes. Add variants to reduce repetition.
